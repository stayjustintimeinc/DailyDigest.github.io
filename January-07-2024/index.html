🌎 WORLD NEWS

Jordan’s King Abdullah II presses Blinken to push for a ceasefire in Gaza

-Al Jazeera English

1. Jordan's King Abdullah II urges US Secretary of State Blinken to push for a ceasefire in Gaza and end the humanitarian crisis.

2. Blinken is on a weeklong trip across the Middle East to calm tensions and avoid a regional escalation of the Israel-Hamas war.

3. The US aims to gather Arab states' views on the future of Gaza before discussing with Israel and emphasizes the need to maximize assistance to those in need.

Ground zero of Japan’s earthquake zone is ravaged by destruction and fire

-NBC News

1. The fishing town of Wajima in Japan has been severely destroyed by a 7.6-magnitude earthquake, with buildings and houses crumbled and a historic market wiped out.

2. The earthquake caused significant damage to roads, landslides, and forced evacuations due to the tsunami warning. Many residents are elderly and may not have the resources or energy to rebuild.

3. The landscape has also been altered, with the ground being shifted upwards and sideways, affecting the local port and creating new beach areas. The death toll has reached 126, with more still trapped or unaccounted for in the rubble.

Post Office scandal: Rishi Sunak considers measures to clear all victims

-BBC.com

1. The UK government is considering measures to clear the names of over 700 sub-postmasters convicted in the Post Office Horizon scandal.

2. Options being reviewed include stripping the Post Office of its role in the appeals process, with the Crown Prosecution Service potentially stepping in to handle appeals.

3. Victims of the scandal are still fighting to have their convictions overturned and to secure full compensation for the financial losses they incurred due to faulty software.

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

FAA Orders Some Boeing Max 737 Planes Grounded Pending Inspection

-The New York Times

1. The Federal Aviation Administration ordered U.S. airlines to inspect and ground Boeing 737 Max 9 planes after a piece of the plane's body came off during a flight, causing terrifying moments for passengers.

2. Alaska and United Airlines canceled flights and grounded their Max 9 fleets for inspection following the incident.

3. The incident was caused by the tearing off of a "mid-cabin door plug," prompting investigations into the plane's maintenance records and the pressurization system.

Trial canceled in Haslam family suit vs. Berkshire Hathaway over truck-stop company value

-CNBC

1. The billion-dollar trial involving Berkshire Hathaway and the Haslam family has been canceled in Delaware Chancery Court.

2. The trial was supposed to determine if Berkshire Hathaway improperly used an accounting method that would short-change the Haslam family in the purchase of Pilot Travel Centers.

3. It is unclear why the trial was canceled and if a settlement has been reached between Berkshire Hathaway and the Haslams.

Exclusive: Alphabet's Isomorphic inks first pharma deals with Eli Lilly and Novartis for $83M upfront, $2.9B in milestones

-Endpoints News

1. Alphabet's AI startup, Isomorphic Labs, has signed partnerships with Eli Lilly and Novartis in deals potentially worth $3 billion.

2. Isomorphic Labs is applying Google DeepMind's biology research, particularly its protein-structure predicting model Alphafold, to drug discovery.

3. Isomorphic Labs, led by CEO Demis Hassabis, is quickly becoming a leading figure in the fast-growing AI biotech space.

6 Best Smart Shades, Blinds, and Curtains (2023)

-WIRED

1. Smart shades and automatic curtains are becoming more affordable and accessible, offering practical benefits such as privacy, temperature regulation, and convenience.

Microsoft unveils 'Copilot' key, its biggest update to Windows keyboard in 30 years

-KSL.com

1. Microsoft is introducing a "Copilot" key on its Windows keyboard, which will summon an AI-powered assistant.

2. The Copilot key will simplify the computing experience by allowing users to engage with the AI chatbot for assistance with tasks like drafting emails.

3. The new key features a Copilot ribbon logo and marks the first major change to the Windows PC keyboard in three decades.
