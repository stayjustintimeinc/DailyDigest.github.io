FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day, August 31st, in 2004, NASA's spacecraft MESSENGER (MErcury Surface, Space ENvironment, GEochemistry, and Ranging) successfully made its first flyby of planet Venus. This maneuver helped the spacecraft to adjust its trajectory and gain gravitational assistance, ultimately enabling it to reach its final destination: the planet Mercury. Launched in 2004 and arriving at Mercury in 2011, MESSENGER became the first spacecraft to orbit the innermost planet of our solar system.

At Least 74 Killed in Johannesburg Building Fire, Including Children: Live Updates

-The New York Times

1. At least 74 people, including 12 children, died in a residential fire in Johannesburg, South Africa's worst fire in history.

2. The building was overcrowded, neglected, and became a death trap due to security gates, garbage, and flimsy subdividing.

3. The city has over 600 derelict, illegally occupied structures, exacerbating the housing shortage and economic crisis in the country.

Philippines, Taiwan, Malaysia reject China's latest South China Sea map

-Reuters

1. The Philippines, Malaysia, Taiwan, and Vietnam have rejected a map released by China that claims sovereignty over the South China Sea. They called on China to act responsibly and abide by international law.

2. China's map, which includes a U-shaped line covering about 90% of the South China Sea, has been deemed baseless and without legal grounds by the Philippines and Malaysia. Vietnam also stated that China's claims based on the map have no value and violate Vietnamese and international laws.

3. The map released by China is different from previous versions and includes a line with 10 dashes, encompassing Taiwan. Taiwan has rejected China's claim and stated that it is "absolutely not a part of the People's Republic of China."

New video shows Wagner's Prigozhin joke about death before plane crash

3. The plane crash, which killed Prigozhin and other Wagner Group members, is still under investigation. Some speculate that it was an intentional act ordered by Russian President Vladimir Putin as revenge for the group's rebellion against Moscow. Eurozone Inflation Holds Steady at 5.3 Percent

-The New York Times

1. Eurozone consumer prices rose 5.3 percent in August, defying expectations for a slowdown.

2. Food inflation and energy costs were the main contributors to the inflation rate.

3. The rise in inflation in some of the region's largest economies raises questions about potential interest rate hikes by the European Central Bank.

Lululemon sees sales and profit jump 18%, boosts full-year guidance

-CNBC

1. Lululemon reported an 18% increase in both sales and profit for its fiscal second quarter, surpassing Wall Street's estimates.

2. The athletic apparel retailer raised its full-year sales and profit guidance, expecting sales to be between $9.51 billion and $9.57 billion, and profits to be between $12.02 to $12.17 per share.

3. Lululemon's second fiscal quarter net income was $341.6 million, or $2.68 per share, with sales reaching $2.21 billion, up 18% from the previous year. However, it fell slightly short of same store sales expectations.
