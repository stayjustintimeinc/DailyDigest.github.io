FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On December 19, 1919, the iconic toy company, Parker Brothers, purchased the rights to a game called "Monopoly" from its creator, Elizabeth Magie. Originally named "The Landlord's Game," Magie had designed it as a critique of monopolistic practices and the negative consequences of capitalism. However, Parker Brothers transformed it into the beloved classic board game we know today, bringing joy to millions of families worldwide.

🌎 WORLD NEWS

Iceland volcano: Pollution warning for capital after eruption

-BBC.com

1. Volcano eruption in Iceland's Reykjanes peninsula threatens gas pollution in the capital.

2. Fumes could reach Reykjavik by Tuesday evening or Wednesday morning.

3. Authorities are preparing for potential lava flows that could destroy homes and infrastructure.

China earthquake: At least 118 killed after 6.2 magnitude quake hit northwestern China

-The Associated Press

1. A magnitude 6.2 earthquake struck a cold and mountainous region in northwestern China, resulting in a death toll of at least 100 people.

2. The earthquake caused the collapse of houses and buildings, leaving behind significant amounts of debris.

3. Residents in the affected area, including students, have been evacuated and are now seeking shelter in tents provided by the government.

Putin says Russia ready to hold talks on the future of Ukraine

-FRANCE 24 English

1. Russian President Vladimir Putin expressed willingness to hold talks with Ukraine, the United States, and Europe regarding the future of Ukraine, but emphasized that Russia will defend its national interests.

2. Putin stated that Russia would not give up what it considers to be its territory and that it does not intend to engage in conflict with Europe.

3. Russia has increased its military capabilities, including production of tanks, unmanned aerial vehicles, and artillery shells, and has laid significant minefields and anti-tank defenses in Ukraine. Putin also stated that Russian forces now have the initiative on the battlefield.

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

Google users will share $630 million in a Play store settlement

-CNN

1. Google has agreed to pay $700 million in an antitrust settlement with US states and consumers regarding its Play app store.

2. Approximately 102 million US consumers will receive a total of $630 million in compensation, while the remaining $70 million will be paid to the participating states.

3. The settlement requires Google to make changes to its app store practices, including allowing users to choose between its proprietary billing system and third-party payment channels, and simplifying the process of installing apps from unofficial sources.

You can still grab an Apple Watch Series 9 for $329 before Apple halts sales

-The Verge

1. Apple is halting sales of the Apple Watch Series 9 and Ultra 2 in the US due to a patent dispute, starting on December 21st at 3PM ET.

2. Both wearables are currently in stock and on sale, with the Apple Watch Series 9 starting at $329 and the Ultra 2 starting at $699 when you clip the on-page coupon.

3. The Apple Watch Series 9 and Ultra 2 are fitness trackers for iPhone users, with features such as a second-gen ultra wideband chip, onboard Siri processing, and a customizable Action Button.

Nikola Founder Trevor Milton Sentenced to Four Years for Fraud

-Yahoo Finance

1. Trevor Milton, founder of Nikola Corp., has been sentenced to four years in prison for lying to shareholders about the company's progress.

2. Milton was convicted of securities fraud and wire fraud for misrepresenting key details about the development of Nikola's products and technology.

3. The government claimed that Milton's fraud cost investors $660 million, and Milton has been ordered to pay a $1 million fine.

🤖 TECH NEWS

TomTom and Microsoft team up to bring generative AI to automobiles

-Engadget

1. TomTom has announced the development of a fully integrated, AI-powered conversational automotive assistant that will offer sophisticated voice interaction and natural conversation capabilities for navigation and control of onboard systems.

2. The AI assistant is developed in partnership with Microsoft and leverages OpenAI's large language models, Azure Cosmos DB, and Azure Cognitive Services to provide advanced functionalities.

3. TomTom aims to integrate the voice assistant into various interfaces offered by major automobile manufacturers, with the technology being initially showcased at CES in January.

Beeper's effort to keep iMessage on Android keeps getting more complex

-The Verge

1. Beeper claims to have a new fix that allows users to send iMessages from their Android phones by creating "unique" registration data to bypass Apple's restrictions.

2. The fix requires owning or having easy access to a trusted Mac, as the registration data needs to be periodically refreshed by turning on the Mac regularly.

3. Beeper plans to open source its iMessage bridge and the tool for creating registration data, but Apple may still find ways to shut down Beeper's ability to send iMessages in the future.

Next iPad Pro Might Support MagSafe Charging

-MacRumors

1. The next-generation iPad Pro set to release in 2024 might include support for MagSafe charging.

2. Apple was considering wireless charging capabilities for the iPad back in 2021.

3. The 2024 iPad Pro models are expected to have design updates and feature OLED displays.
