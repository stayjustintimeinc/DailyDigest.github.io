Israel-Hamas War Live: Latest Updates and Gaza News

-The New York Times

1. The United Nations Security Council has postponed a vote on a resolution calling for a halt in fighting and increased aid deliveries in the Israel-Hamas war in Gaza. The delay was due to the United States requesting more time for negotiations.

2. The main point of contention is whether the United Nations should take over the inspection of aid shipments to Gaza from Israel. The US and Israel object to this proposal.

3. The United Arab Emirates and Egypt have advocated for a UN inspection system to improve the efficiency of aid delivery, but Israel is resistant to relinquishing control of the inspection process.

India's Parliament votes on key bills with over 140 opposition members suspended

-NPR

1. Dozens of opposition lawmakers in India were suspended after holding a protest demanding a statement on a security breach in Parliament.

2. Turmoil in Indian politics escalated with smoke bombs at Parliament and the suspension of more than 140 opposition lawmakers.

3. The suspended legislators were unable to participate in the voting on key bills, preventing the representation of nearly 140 million voters.

"They've left me behind," American Paul Whelan says from Russian prison after failed bid to secure release

-CBS News

1. American citizen Paul Whelan, imprisoned in Russia on espionage charges, criticizes the Biden administration for leaving him behind while other Americans have been freed in prisoner swaps.

2. Whelan expresses frustration and fears being left out of any future prisoner swap with Russia, stating that his life is draining away while negotiations continue.

3. The U.S. government has classified Whelan as wrongfully detained and stated that Russia rejected significant proposals for his release, leaving his family uncertain about the progress of his case.

Dow closes more than 470 points lower Wednesday to snap 9-day win streak: Live updates

-CNBC

1. Stocks tumbled on Wednesday, with the Dow Jones Industrial Average sliding 475.92 points, marking its worst session since October.

Nippon Steel's Harvard-educated president explains his $14 billion deal for U.S. Steel: It's all about China and leading the 'free-competition world'

-Yahoo Finance

1. Nippon Steel plans to buy U.S. Steel for over $14 billion, creating a new steelmaking giant that would dominate the sector outside of China.

2. The deal includes Nippon Steel paying a premium price of $55 a share for U.S. Steel, significantly above its market price.

3. The decision to buy U.S. Steel is facing backlash from U.S. lawmakers and the United Steelworkers union, who are concerned about American security and the violation of an agreement with the union. The deal still requires approval from the Committee on Foreign Investment in the United States.

General Mills' price hikes backfire as consumers say no more

-Star Tribune

1. General Mills is experiencing a decline in sales as consumers buy fewer of their products, potentially due to high prices.

2. The company predicts that sales may not grow at all until next summer but believes that advertising and product innovations will lead to better results in the future.

3. General Mills plans to trim costs and streamline manufacturing processes in order to offset inflation and improve profitability.
