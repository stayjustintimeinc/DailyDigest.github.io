Complex cave rescue looms in Turkey as American Mark Dickey stuck 3,200 feet inside Morca cave

-CBS News

1. An American speleologist named Mark Dickey has become ill and is trapped at a depth of over 3,280 feet in a cave in southern Turkey. He has gastrointestinal bleeding and is unable to leave the cave on his own.

2. Turkish and international cave rescue experts, including doctors and paramedics, are working on a plan to hoist Dickey out of the cave system. His condition has stabilized and he is in good spirits.

3. The rescue operation involves more than 170 people, including rescue teams from Hungary, Bulgaria, Italy, Croatia, and Poland. The cave is being prepared for Dickey's safe extraction, and the process could take several days with rest points along the way.

Manhunt launched for terror suspect who escaped London prison

-NPR

1. Daniel Abed Khalife, a former soldier accused of terrorism offenses, escaped from London's Wandsworth Prison by attaching himself to the undercarriage of a food delivery truck.

2. A manhunt is underway as Khalife is considered a potential flight risk, leading to increased security measures at ports and airports in the United Kingdom.

3. Questions have been raised about why a terrorism suspect was held in a medium-security facility and an independent investigation will be conducted to determine the reasons for Khalife's escape.

Russia-Ukraine War Live Updates: Southern Russian Region Rocked By Explosions

-The New York Times

1. Russian air defenses shoot down two drones in and around Rostov-on-Don, home to Russia's southern military headquarters. Ukrainian response unknown.

2. Explosions occur near one of Russia's major military hubs in Rostov-on-Don following drone attacks. One person injured, with damage to cars and buildings.

3. Ukraine has recently intensified its assaults inside Russia, employing various weapons and targeting military facilities across the country.
