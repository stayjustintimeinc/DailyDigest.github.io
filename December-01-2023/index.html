FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day, December 1, in 1955, a historic event took place. Rosa Parks, a courageous African American woman, refused to give up her seat to a white passenger on a crowded bus in Montgomery, Alabama. Her act of defiance and refusal to abide by the racially segregated seating laws sparked the Montgomery Bus Boycott, marking a pivotal moment in the Civil Rights Movement. Rosa Parks' courageous stand continues to inspire and serve as a symbol of resistance against injustice to this day.
