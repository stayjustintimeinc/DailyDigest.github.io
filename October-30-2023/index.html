FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day in history, October 30th, back in 1938, the infamous "War of the Worlds" radio broadcast took place. This radio drama, directed by Orson Welles and performed by the Mercury Theatre on the Air, was based on H. G. Wells' novel about a Martian invasion. The realistic format of the show, narrated as a series of news bulletins, caused widespread panic among listeners who mistook it for an actual alien invasion. While it was mainly a fictional exercise, the episode serves as a notable reminder of the influence of media and the power of storytelling.

🌎 WORLD NEWS

German-Israeli woman taken hostage by Hamas at music festival is dead, Israel says

-Reuters

1. A German-Israeli woman, Shani Nicole Louk, who was kidnapped by Hamas during a music festival, has been confirmed murdered and dead.

2. Louk was one of many people taken by Hamas during an assault on Israeli communities, resulting in the deaths of 1,400 people.

3. The circumstances surrounding her death are unclear, but her family believes she was killed on the same day she was abducted.

Israeli Forces Make Major Advance Toward Gaza City

-The Wall Street Journal

1. Israeli tanks and infantry have advanced into the outskirts of Gaza City in an effort to encircle the largest population center in Gaza.

2. This move is part of Israel's ongoing war against Hamas, aimed at eradicating the Islamist militant group.

3. Hamas fighters have previously carried out attacks inside Israel, resulting in numerous casualties and hostages.

Israeli troops advance in Gaza, ‘impossible’ hospital evacuation and regional warnings: What to know Monday

-CNN

1. Israeli troops have advanced over two miles into Gaza as part of their ground operation against Hamas.

2. Hospitals in Gaza are facing difficulties due to airstrikes and orders to evacuate patients.

3. UN officials warn of a breakdown in civil order in Gaza and emphasize the urgent need for a humanitarian ceasefire and aid delivery.

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

UAW, GM sign tentative deal that will end strike if workers ratify it

-The Washington Post

1. The United Auto Workers (UAW) has reached a tentative contract deal with GM, making it the last of the Big Three automakers to come to an agreement, which would end the historic six-week strike.

2. The GM deal follows similar agreements with Ford and Stellantis, bringing significant gains for workers, including a 25% raise in base wages over 4½ years.

3. The resolution of the strikes could benefit President Biden and his pro-union stance, as well as stabilize the auto industry, which contributes 3% to the nation's GDP.

Cross examination of Sam Bankman-Fried begins in FTX fraud trial - live

-The Guardian

1. In the cross examination, the prosecutor contrasts Sam Bankman-Fried's public statements about FTX being transparent and safe with his private disparagement of regulators.

2. Bankman-Fried appeared stilted and potentially unlikeable to the jurors as the prosecutor questioned his intentions towards customers and his public statements about the safety of the FTX platform.

3. The prosecutor also questioned Bankman-Fried's persona and his ability to tell a good story to potential investors, suggesting that his unkempt appearance and statements may have been an act.

FDA approves Junshi, Coherus’ PD-1 toripalimab for nasopharyngeal carcinoma

-Endpoints News

1. The FDA has approved toripalimab, the first Chinese PD-1 drug, for recurrent or metastatic nasopharyngeal carcinoma (NPC).

2. Junshi BioSciences developed the drug and Coherus BioSciences acquired the US and Canada rights.

3. The drug will be marketed as Loqtorzi in the US and is expected to launch in the first quarter of 2024.
