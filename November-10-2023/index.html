🌎 WORLD NEWS

Turmoil in UK government after key minister slams police ahead of pro-Palestinian march on Armistice Day

-CNN

1. The British government is anticipating a challenging weekend, with conflicting events scheduled on the same day - Armistice Day commemorations and a pro-Palestinian protest in London.

2. Prime Minister Rishi Sunak initially wanted to prevent the protest, fearing vandalism to the Cenotaph monument, but had to backtrack due to the importance of freedom of protest.

3. Sunak and his Home Secretary Suella Braverman have expressed criticism toward the protest, with Braverman accusing the police of bias and comparing the march to supporting Northern Irish terror groups in the past. This has caused speculation about Braverman's future and questions about Sunak's leadership authority.

Species of mammal named after David Attenborough believed extinct rediscovered

-The Guardian

1. Long-lost species of mammal, the long-beaked echidna, with a unique combination of features was rediscovered in Indonesia's Cyclops Mountains after more than 60 years.

2. The echidna, named after David Attenborough, was photographed for the first time in decades by a trail camera on the last day of an expedition led by Oxford University scientists.

3. Echidnas are shy, nocturnal burrow-dwellers and belong to the monotremes, an egg-laying group of mammals that separated from the rest of the mammal tree-of-life around 200 million years ago.

Israel: People in Gaza can use a 'humanitarian corridor' for several hours a day

-NPR

1. Israel agrees to allow civilians from northern Gaza to move safely to southern areas for a few hours each day, creating a humanitarian corridor.

2. Pauses for safe evacuations have been implemented before, with tens of thousands of Palestinians using them in the past week.

3. Talks for a longer-term cease-fire or humanitarian pause are ongoing, with discussions on the release of hostages being held between U.S. officials and leaders in the Middle East.

What is the impact of quantum computing on current encryption methods?
