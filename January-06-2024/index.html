FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day, January 5th, in 1925, the world-renowned Nellie Tayloe Ross was inaugurated as the 14th Governor of Wyoming, becoming the first female governor in the history of the United States. She succeeded her late husband, who had held the position before his passing. Nellie Tayloe Ross broke barriers and paved the way for future female leaders, leaving an indelible mark on American politics.

🌎 WORLD NEWS

Israel tries to appease US with proposals for Gaza as Blinken heads to Middle East

-The Guardian

1. Israeli officials are offering policy proposals on Gaza to address mounting frustration in Washington ahead of a meeting between US Secretary of State Antony Blinken and Benjamin Netanyahu.

2. Critics argue that the proposals lack detail and commitment, with concerns about the absence of a clear plan for governance in Gaza and reluctance to engage in the US's preferred options.

3. The US is pressuring Netanyahu to protect civilians in Gaza, increase aid to the territory, and rein in far-right ministers' rhetoric. Israel is also facing calls to shift towards more targeted military operations in Gaza.

Defense Secretary Lloyd Austin recovering after being admitted to hospital

-CNN

1. Defense Secretary Lloyd Austin was admitted to Walter Reed National Military Medical Center for complications from an elective medical procedure.

2. The Pentagon announced Austin's hospitalization four days after he was initially admitted.

3. Austin is expected to resume his full duties later Friday, but it is unclear when he will be released from the hospital.

US sets final food can steel dumping duties on China, Canada, Germany, Korea

-Reuters

1. The U.S. Commerce Department has determined that tin mill products from Canada, China, Germany, and South Korea are being dumped onto the U.S. market.

2. Tin mill products from China are also found to be subsidized, resulting in countervailing anti-subsidy duties.

3. Imports of tin mill products from the Netherlands, Taiwan, Turkey, and the United Kingdom are not being dumped, according to the Commerce Department's findings.

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

Global shipping delays cast shadow over strong December jobs report

-CNBC

1. The December jobs report showed strong economic growth, with employers adding 216,000 jobs and the unemployment rate remaining steady at 3.7%.

2. Danish shipping company Maersk announced that it will continue diverting its fleet from the Red Sea indefinitely due to ongoing attacks by Houthi rebels, potentially causing global shipping delays.

3. The White House is concerned about the impact of shipping holdups on the U.S. supply chain and the economy, considering past disruptions during the pandemic.

Elon Musk calls out Mark Cuban over DEI: 'His hypocrisy convinces no one'

-New York Post

1. Elon Musk criticizes Mark Cuban for his support of diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) initiatives.

2. Musk argues that DEI is just another form of racism and discrimination.

3. The online feud between the two billionaires began after a user questioned Cuban about discrimination against Asians in college admissions.

SpaceX in new lawsuit claims NLRB structure violates Constitution

-The Hill

1. SpaceX sues the National Labor Relations Board (NLRB) over alleged unconstitutional structure.

2. NLRB filed a complaint against SpaceX for alleged unlawful firings.

3. SpaceX seeks to dismiss the complaint and demands a trial by jury.

🤖 TECH NEWS

Samsung Unpacked Event: What to Expect From Galaxy S24 Phones

-CNET

1. Samsung is set to unveil its Galaxy S24 lineup at the Galaxy Unpacked event on Jan. 17, with new AI features expected to be a key focus.

2. The Galaxy S24 series will likely receive minor upgrades in design, camera, and processor, but the main changes will come in the form of new AI software and features.

3. Samsung's upcoming event will showcase its "most intelligent mobile experience yet," with AI-powered features like on-device translation and a focus on AI integration in photography.

Discontinued and unreleased Microsoft peripherals revived by licensing deal

-Ars Technica

1. Onward Brands will give Microsoft-branded computer peripherals a new life under new branding. They will be rebranded as Incase products with "Designed by Microsoft" branding.

2. Incase plans to bring back 23 Microsoft-designed products in 2024, including keyboards, mice, webcams, headsets, and speakers. They also plan to release an ergonomic keyboard that Microsoft designed but never released.

3. Microsoft will still retain ownership of its designs and may continue to renew its license to Incase in the future. Some classic Microsoft peripherals, like the Intellimouse, are not included in the Incase reboot list.

Kohler’s voice-controlled bidet seat turns your dumb toilet into a luxurious smart-throne

-Engadget

1. Kohler introduces the PureWash E930 Bidet Seat, which transforms a regular toilet into a smart toilet powered by Alexa or Google Assistant.

2. The bidet seat has various features including hands-free opening and closing, self-cleaning, adjustable water temperature and pressure, and programmable presets.

3. The PureWash E930 is available for order with a white color option now, and a black color option will be available later in February.
