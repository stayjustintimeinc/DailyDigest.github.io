Biden admin imposes new sanctions on Iran for anniversary of Mahsa Amini’s death

-CNN

1. The Biden administration has imposed sanctions on more than two dozen Iranian individuals and entities connected to the violent suppression of protests following the death of Mahsa Amini one year ago.

2. The sanctions target key members of Iran's security forces, the Law Enforcement Forces, individuals and companies involved in censorship and internet blocking, and IRGC and regime-controlled media outlets.

3. The US State Department is also imposing visa restrictions on 13 Iranian officials for their involvement in the detention, killing, or inhibition of rights of peaceful protestors. The Biden administration stands with the citizens of Iran in their fight for a free and democratic future.

Chinese defence minister under investigation for corrupt procurement

-Reuters

1. China's Defence Minister Li Shangfu is under investigation by Chinese authorities for potential procurement of military equipment.

2. Eight senior officials from the Chinese military's procurement unit, led by Li Shangfu from 2017 to 2022, are also under investigation.

3. The investigation is being carried out by the military's powerful disciplinary inspection commission and follows previous leadership changes in China's military and foreign ministry.

-NPR

2. Since the financial crisis, lenders have embraced safer loans, making it more secure for homebuyers to obtain mortgages.

💡 Q&A OF THE DAY

What is the tallest mountain in the world when measured from base to summit, rather than from sea level?

The tallest mountain in the world when measured from base to summit is Mauna Kea in Hawaii. While its elevation above sea level is only 13,796 feet (4,205 meters), the mountain extends about 19,700 feet (6,000 meters) below the water's surface. When measured from its base on the ocean floor, Mauna Kea has a height of over 33,000 feet (about 10,000 meters), taller than Mount Everest if measured in the same way.
