🌎 WORLD NEWS

Pope Francis insists Europe doesn't have a 'migrant emergency'

-Euronews

1. Pope Francis urges European leaders, including French President Emmanuel Macron, to open ports to migrants and deal with migration in a humane manner.

2. The pope criticizes the closing of doors to migrants and emphasizes the importance of responding with charity and ensuring legal and regular entrances.

3. This visit comes at a time when migration to Europe is making headlines and Francis challenges the notion of an "emergency," stating that migration is a long-term reality that requires a European response.

Ukraine launched another attack on Sevastopol in Crimea

-The Associated Press

1. Ukraine launched another missile attack on Sevastopol on the occupied Crimean Peninsula, causing air raid alerts and disruptions to ferry traffic.

2. Ukraine's intelligence chief claimed that at least nine people were killed and 16 wounded in the previous attack on the headquarters of Russia's Black Sea Fleet.

3. The Ukrainian military conducted 12 strikes on the Black Sea Fleet headquarters, hitting anti-aircraft missile systems and artillery units.

Taiwan factory fire leaves at least 5 dead, more than 100 injured

-ABC News

1. A fire and explosions at a golf ball factory in southern Taiwan have resulted in at least five deaths and over 100 injuries.

2. Five people are still missing following the incident, while three firefighters were among the deceased.

3. Taiwan's President Tsai Ing-wen expressed her condolences and plans to visit the affected area, while the cause of the fire is still under investigation.

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

‘Rupert Murdoch is a symptom’: Fox’s future politics look the same as past

-The Guardian

1. Analysts predict little change in the political bias and disinformation at Fox News, even with new leadership, as they are likely to continue amplifying outrage, division, and support for conservative causes.

2. The recent settlement with Dominion Voting Systems and the ongoing defamation lawsuit from Smartmatic could have significant implications for Fox News, potentially causing reputational and economic harm.

3. Despite stepping down, Rupert Murdoch is expected to continue wielding power in News Corp and Fox, as he stated his ongoing engagement with news and ideas.

Biden to walk the picket line in Michigan to support UAW strikers

-CNN

1. President Joe Biden will visit Michigan on Tuesday, joining the picket line with United Auto Workers union members.

2. Biden's visit highlights the ongoing strike against the nation's largest automakers and the political opportunity it presents.

3. Former President Donald Trump criticized Biden's plans, claiming credit for Biden's decision to visit the autoworkers and emphasizing the importance of UAW's endorsement.

As CD Rates Top 7%, Financial Planners Explain: Save Cash or Invest?

-Business Insider

1. Financial advisors caution against relying too heavily on high-yield savings accounts and CDs for retirement portfolios, as the growth potential and returns from diversified investments often surpass those of these "safe" accounts.

2. The risks of keeping money in savings accounts and CDs include inflation eroding the real value of the funds and missing out on potentially higher returns in the stock market or other avenues.

3. The opportunity cost of locking money in CDs can be high, as it limits the ability to take advantage of investment opportunities or market rebounds, and early withdrawal penalties may result in losing gains. Additionally, considering tax considerations is important, as investment gains in the stock market may have more favorable tax treatment compared to interest earned on savings accounts and CDs.

'I am sorry': Unity partially walks back on controversial monetization plans | VGC

-Video Games Chronicle

1. Unity has revised its monetization plans for its game engine following backlash from developers.

2. The proposed Unity Runtime Fee, which would have charged developers for each installation of a game using Unity engine, will no longer apply to Unity Personal or Plus plans.

3. The fee will only be applicable to Unity Pro and Unity Enterprise plans for new games created or upgraded to the Long Term Support version in 2024 or later, after reaching a revenue of $1,000,000 and 1,000,000 initial engagements.

People are dragging Apple's 'FineWoven' iPhone cases

-Business Insider

1. Apple's FineWoven iPhone cases, touted as an eco-friendly replacement for leather cases, have received negative reviews from customers.

2. Reviewers have criticized the FineWoven case for being easily scratched, attracting dust, and leaving imprints from MagSafe charging.

3. The FineWoven cases have low ratings on Amazon, and some customers have expressed disappointment on Reddit, while others have found the case to be soft but potentially less durable than leather.
