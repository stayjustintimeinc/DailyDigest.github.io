FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day, December 16th, in 1773, the Boston Tea Party took place. A group of American colonists disguised as Native Americans boarded three British ships and dumped 342 chests of tea into the Boston Harbor as a protest against the Tea Act, which they considered as taxation without representation. This event played a significant role in the lead up to the American Revolution.

🌎 WORLD NEWS

U.S. says its destroyer shot down 14 drones in Red Sea launched from Yemen

-CBS News

1. An American destroyer successfully shot down 14 drones launched from Huthi-controlled areas of Yemen in the Red Sea, with no damage to ships or reported injuries.

2. The Huthi rebels in Yemen have been launching drone and missile strikes targeting Israel, resulting in casualties and kidnappings.

3. Due to the escalating threat to shipping in the area, container shipping giant Maersk has ordered ships to halt voyages and Mediterranean Shipping Company has rerouted its shipping services to go through the Cape of Good Hope.

NY Times latest Bombshell: Israel (and U.S.) ignored the money fueling the Hamas attack for years

-Daily Kos

1. The article discusses how Hamas funded its October 7 attack on Israel and how it purchased weapons, supplies, and carried out training.

2. Israeli security officials obtained secret documents in 2018 revealing a private equity fund Hamas used to finance its operations, which included assets worth hundreds of millions of dollars.

3. Despite having access to this information, no actions were taken to sanction the companies listed in the documents or pressure Turkey, the hub of the financial network, to shut it down. This highlights a failure in priorities and the missed opportunity to disrupt Hamas's financial network.

Iran executes 'Mossad agent' convicted of spying for Israel

-FRANCE 24 English

1. A man was executed in Iran after being convicted of working with Israel's intelligence services.

2. The man was found guilty of intelligence cooperation, espionage, and providing classified information to the Mossad spy service.

3. Iran has previously arrested alleged agents working for foreign countries, including Israel, with whom it does not have diplomatic relations.

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

Lead contamination in applesauce possibly ‘economically motivated’, says FDA

-The Guardian

1. The FDA is investigating a food facility in Ecuador in connection to elevated levels of lead found in cinnamon applesauce that has caused illnesses in children.

2. The contaminated applesauce brands that have been recalled include WanaBana, Schnucks, and Weis.

3. The FDA is working with Ecuadorian authorities to inspect the cinnamon supplier and requesting more authority from Congress to address the issue of food contamination.

Activision Blizzard to Pay More Than $50 Million to Settle Lawsuit That Spurred Microsoft's Takeover

-The Wall Street Journal

1. Activision Blizzard has agreed to pay over $50 million to settle a lawsuit filed by a California regulator. The lawsuit alleged that the company's leadership ignored employee complaints of sexual harassment, discrimination, and pay disparity.

2. The settlement of the lawsuit played a significant role in Microsoft's takeover of Activision Blizzard in October. The lawsuit shed light on the toxic work culture within the company and added pressure on Activision Blizzard's management.

3. The payment of more than $50 million is seen as a step towards addressing the issues raised by the lawsuit and taking responsibility for the company's past misconduct. The settlement reflects a commitment to improving workplace conditions and promoting equality within the organization.

The Federal Reserve could do what was thought impossible: Defeat inflation without a steep recession

-The Associated Press

1. Inflation in the United States is expected to fall below the Federal Reserve's target of 2% in the coming months, thanks to high interest rates and improved supply chains.

2. The cost of goods, such as used cars and furniture, has been falling for six consecutive months, while housing and rental costs are growing more slowly.

3. Despite the positive outlook, officials remain cautious and want to see further evidence of falling inflation before declaring victory.
