Israel-Hamas War: U.S. Vetoes Security Council Resolution Demanding Cease-Fire in Gaza

-The New York Times

1. The United States vetoed a U.N. resolution for an immediate cease-fire in the Israel-Hamas war, arguing that ending the war before dismantling Hamas would lead to future conflict.

2. The U.N. secretary general warned that without a halt in the fighting, it would be difficult to provide aid to the millions of Palestinians trapped in the war zone, who are facing a humanitarian catastrophe.

3. Thirteen members of the U.N. Security Council voted in favor of the cease-fire resolution, while Britain abstained and the United States cast the only no vote.

Six French teenagers convicted in connection with 2020 beheading of teacher Paty

-Reuters

1. A French court convicted six teenagers in connection with the 2020 beheading of teacher Samuel Paty, who showed his students caricatures of the Prophet Mohammad.

2. Among those convicted was a teenage girl who made false accusations and slanderous comments about Paty.

3. The sentences for the convicted teenagers were considered too lenient by some, with the harshest being a 6-month prison sentence served under electronic surveillance.

Thousands of tons of dead sardines wash ashore in northern Japan

-ABC News

1. Thousands of dead sardines have washed up on a beach in northern Japan, with some mackerel also present.

2. The cause of the phenomenon is unknown, but it is speculated that the fish may have been chased by larger fish or become exhausted in densely packed schools and washed ashore.

3. Local residents are advised not to consume the fish, as their decomposing state could affect the marine environment and lower oxygen levels in the water.

Microsoft faces UK antitrust probe over OpenAI deal structure

-Cointelegraph

1. The United Kingdom's antitrust regulator is considering initiating a merger investigation into Microsoft's collaboration with OpenAI.

2. Microsoft declared that it only plays a non-voting observer role on OpenAI's board.

3. The examination will assess whether the collaboration constitutes an "acquisition of control" and involves the substantial influence of one party over another.

Google faces controversy over edited Gemini AI demo video

-CNBC

1. Google is under scrutiny for its demonstration video of its new AI model, Gemini. The video shows Gemini's capabilities in recognizing visual images and engaging in conversations with users.

2. The video includes disclaimers about reduced latency and shortened outputs, but it doesn't explicitly state that it wasn't conducted in real-time. This raises questions about the accuracy of the demonstration.

3. Google later confirmed that the demo used still images and text prompts instead of real-time interactions, which is different from what was initially suggested. The company has not provided further comments on the matter.
