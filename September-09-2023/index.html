UAW strike: How Ford F-150 production and prices could be affected

-NBC News

1. If the United Auto Workers (UAW) union fails to reach a new contract agreement with Ford, General Motors, and Chrysler-maker Stellantis by September 14, workers may go on strike. The entire union, consisting of over 140,000 workers, going on strike for 10 days could cost the U.S. economy $5 billion.

2. There is a possibility that instead of a full strike, a smaller number of UAW workers may walk off the job at certain factories, such as those producing Ford's popular F-150 pickup truck. This tactic aims to create an imbalance in damages and could be sustained for a longer duration at a lower cost to the union.

3. A work stoppage targeting the F-150 could lead to increased vehicle prices, as the demand rises and inventories shrink. This, along with higher interest rates for auto loans, would result in more expensive monthly payments. The strike would have three main effects: workers losing pay, manufacturers losing money, and the broader auto industry losing sales, but it may not be enough to cause a recession.
