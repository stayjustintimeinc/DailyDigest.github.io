🌎 WORLD NEWS

Frederik X is proclaimed the new king of Denmark after his mother Queen Margrethe II abdicates

-The Associated Press

1. Queen Margrethe II of Denmark abdicates the throne, making way for her son Frederik X to become the new king.

2. This is the first time in nearly 900 years that a Danish monarch has abdicated.

3. The abdication ceremony was celebrated by massive crowds, marking the transition of power from a beloved queen to her popular son.

Netanyahu declares ‘no one will stop’ Israel’s war to end Hamas after world court hears genocide allegations

-Fox News

1. Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu declares that "no one will stop Israel" in its war against Hamas.

2. The International Court of Justice held hearings on South Africa's allegations of Israeli genocide against Palestinians in Gaza.

3. Israel rejects South Africa's charge of genocide and defends its right to defend itself against Hamas attacks.

Volcano erupts in Iceland, flowing lava reaches fishing town

-Reuters

1. A volcano erupted in southwest Iceland, causing molten lava flows and reaching the outskirts of a small fishing town.

2. The town was evacuated earlier, and no people were in danger, but some houses were set alight.

3. This is the second volcanic eruption in southwest Iceland in less than a month and the fifth outbreak since 2021.

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

Boeing and U.S. aerospace set back by Alaska Airlines fuselage blowout

-The Seattle Times

1. The recent incident involving the explosion of a door-sized section of a 737 MAX 9 fuselage has further damaged Boeing's reputation, following previous MAX crashes in 2018 and 2019.

2. The cause of the explosion appears to be a mistake in installing and inspecting a door plug, which was assembled by Spirit AeroSystems. Boeing is being held responsible for not overseeing their supplier's work.

3. The incident calls into question the entire aviation manufacturing system and the need for a cultural fix to ensure the production of safe airplanes without life-threatening defects. The FAA and Boeing may face scrutiny and potential leadership changes.

Biotech and pharma companies are betting on a promising class of cancer drugs to drive growth

-CNBC

1. The JPMorgan Healthcare Conference highlighted the growing interest in antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs) as a promising group of cancer drugs.

2. Johnson & Johnson's acquisition of ADC-developer Ambrx Biopharma for $2 billion demonstrates the potential of ADCs in cancer treatment.

3. Pfizer and Merck, among other drugmakers, are also investing in ADCs and see them as key growth drivers for their businesses.

Please stop ignoring your flight attendants

-CNBC

1. The president of the Association of Flight Attendants-CWA emphasizes the need for passengers to pay attention to pre-take-off safety demonstrations on airplanes.

2. Recent airline incidents, such as a collision at Tokyo's Haneda Airport and an incident with an Alaska Airlines Boeing 737 Max 9, highlight the importance of being aware and prepared during flights.

3. Neglecting safety demonstrations can put passengers at risk and hinder their ability to respond effectively in case of emergencies.
