-The Associated Press

U.S. warship intercepted Yemen missiles potentially headed towards Israel

-NPR

1. The Pentagon successfully intercepted three cruise missiles and several drones launched by Houthi forces in Yemen, potentially aimed towards Israel.

2. The incident could be the first US military defense action in support of Israel since the country was attacked by a Palestinian militant group.

3. The US is on high alert for activity from Iranian proxy groups and has warned against escalating tensions in the region.

Britain's Labour deals big by-election blows to PM Sunak

-Reuters UK

1. Britain's Labour Party won two previously safe parliamentary seats, dealing a blow to Prime Minister Rishi Sunak's Conservatives.

2. The defeats suggest a decline in support for the governing party and indicate that Labour may be on track to win power in the next national election.

3. Labour's victories in Mid-Bedfordshire and Tamworth signal a desire for change among voters who are dissatisfied with the current Conservative government.

U.S. Deficit, Pegged at $1.7 Trillion, Effectively Doubled in 2023

-The New York Times

1. The US federal budget deficit nearly doubled in the 2023 fiscal year due to factors such as slumping tax receipts, rising interest rates, and the demand for expiring pandemic relief benefits.

2. The deficit was recorded as $1.7 trillion in 2023, up from $1.37 trillion in 2022. However, accounting changes related to a student loan forgiveness program proposed by President Biden affected the deficit figures for both years.

3. The widening gap between government spending and revenue is concerning as President Biden faces the challenges of securing aid for Israel and Ukraine while grappling with concerns about government spending and the country's ability to finance two wars. Lawmakers have yet to agree on meaningful spending cuts or tax increases.

Prosecutors in Bankman-Fried trial compare defense argument to 'Dumb and Dumber'

-CNBC

3. The government's case alleges that FTX siphoned billions of dollars from customer accounts to cover losses and for personal expenses, leaving customers unable to retrieve their money.

Google makes some changes to the At a Glance widget on the Pixel 8

-Android Central

1. Google's At a Glance widget is adding a new option called "At a store" which displays shopping lists and Google Pay rewards when in supported stores. The widget can determine the store and open shopping lists through scanning QR codes.

2. The At a Glance widget is being redesigned to include all its features in one unified menu, with 21 toggleable options. Users can personalize the content shown and manage sensitive information on the lock screen.

3. The new updates for the At a Glance widget are currently only available on the Pixel 8 and Pixel 8 Pro, with no clear timeline for wider availability. These updates aim to improve the user experience and offer more convenience.

The MacBook boom is over

-Digital Trends

1. MacBook sales have decreased in 2023 after a surge of success in previous years fueled by increased demand during the pandemic.

2. The success of MacBooks in recent years can be attributed to both the increased demand for laptops and the transition to Apple Silicon chips.

3. Apple's lack of new Mac releases and reduced hype around the product indicate a winding down of updates and setting more realistic expectations moving forward.

OnePlus to showcase 3,000 nits OLED screen - SamMobile

-SamMobile - Samsung news

2. The upcoming display is rumored to have a "2K" resolution and a brightness of 3,000 nits, surpassing the OnePlus Open's peak brightness of 2,800 nits.

3. The display from OnePlus and BOE is expected to be featured in the OnePlus 12, which will have a brighter display than the Galaxy S24.
