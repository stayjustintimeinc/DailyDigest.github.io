Strong quake in Philippines triggers tsunami warnings and evacuations

-Reuters

1. A magnitude 7.5 earthquake struck the southern region of Mindanao, triggering tsunami warnings in the Philippines and Japan.

2. The U.S. Tsunami Warning System later stated that the risk of large waves had passed, but the Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology still maintained the threat of a tsunami.

3. Evacuations were ongoing in the Philippines, with no initial reports of significant damage or casualties, although there were observed waves of 40 cm in Japan.

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

BMW recalls SUVs after Takata air bag inflator blows apart, hurling shrapnel and injuring driver

-ABC News

1. BMW is recalling 486 SUVs in the U.S. due to faulty Takata air bag inflators that can explode in a crash, potentially causing harm to occupants.

2. This recall raises concerns about the safety of the approximately 30 million Takata inflators currently under investigation by the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration.

3. The BMW vehicles involved in the recall are from the 2014 model year and have a moisture absorbing chemical called a dessicant that was not part of previous recalls.

Walmart joins growing list of companies to suspend advertising on Elon Musk's X

-Business Insider

1. Walmart has decided to stop advertising on Elon Musk's social media platform, X (formerly known as Twitter), due to finding other platforms that better reach their customers.

2. Several major companies, including Disney, Apple, IBM, and Warner Bros, have also pulled their ads from X following Musk's apparent endorsement of an antisemitic post.

3. In response to Walmart's decision, Musk made a dig at the retailer and warned that big advertisers abandoning X could "kill" the platform. The controversy could result in a loss of up to $75 million in ad revenue for X by 2023.

US commerce chief warns against China ‘threat’

-South China Morning Post

1. US Commerce Secretary Gina Raimondo calls on lawmakers, Silicon Valley, and US allies to prevent China's acquisition of semiconductors and cutting-edge technologies essential for national security.

2. Raimondo emphasizes China as the biggest threat to the US and emphasizes that protecting national security is more important than short-term revenue for chip companies.

3. Raimondo highlights the need for tighter export controls and increased enforcement, while also advocating for better funding to effectively carry out the mission of protecting America's technological leadership.

🤖 TECH NEWS

Here's what's actually going on in that viral 'glitch in the matrix' iPhone mirror picture

-9to5Mac

1. A viral image showing a comedian seemingly holding three different poses at the same time has sparked speculation and theories as to how it was captured.

2. The image was taken with the panoramic feature on the comedian's iPhone 12, likely by mistake, causing the arms in different positions to be stitched together.

3. Although the image didn't come out wide enough to be designated as a panorama, it was a failed panoramic shot and not a result of Photoshop or manipulation.

4 Months Later, the Pixel Fold Shows Google Needs to Do More

-CNET

1. The Pixel Fold is a beautiful device but has slow software updates compared to Samsung and OnePlus.

2. The Pixel Fold has a limited flexibility issue, as it cannot lie completely flat when unfolded.

3. The device has a distinguished look on the outside but chunky bezels and a noticeable crease on the inside screen.

Cops Warn About New iPhone Feature, Experts Say to Chill

-Newser

1. Apple's latest iOS update introduces a new NameDrop feature that allows users to quickly share contact information with nearby Apple devices by placing them close together.

2. Law enforcement officials in various states have issued warnings about the NameDrop feature, expressing concerns about potential privacy and security risks.

3. Experts argue that the concerns may be exaggerated since both users have to be in close proximity and give consent for contact information sharing to occur. However, caution is still encouraged, and users can disable the NameDrop option in settings.
