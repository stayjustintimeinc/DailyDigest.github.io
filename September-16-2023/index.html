🌎 WORLD NEWS

14 dead as plane carrying tourists crashes in Brazil

-CNN

1. A plane crash in Barcelos, Brazil has resulted in the deaths of 14 people, including tourists.

2. The plane was carrying 12 tourists, a pilot, and a co-pilot, and there are no survivors.

3. The crash was attributed to bad weather, with heavy rain present during the landing.

PLA Submarine ‘Missing’, China’s Defense Minister Underground, Speculations On Mishap Reignites

-EurAsian Times

1. Unconfirmed reports suggest a Chinese nuclear-powered submarine encountered a serious accident in the Yellow Sea, with all hands lost.

2. China's Defense Minister, Li Shangfu, has been absent from the public eye for two weeks, indicating potential turmoil within the ruling Communist Party.

3. The Taiwan Ministry of National Defense has changed its stance, refusing to confirm or deny the submarine mishap, suggesting sensitivity and secrecy surrounding the incident.

Mahsa Amini: Protesters mark one year since death of Iranian student

-BBC

1. The death of Mahsa Amini sparked widespread protests in Iran and around the world.

2. Iranian authorities detained and warned Amjad Amini, Mahsa's father, against marking the anniversary of her death.

3. Family members of other people killed during the protests have also been arrested or threatened in Iran.

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

California sues oil companies claiming they downplayed the risk of fossil fuels

-The Guardian US

1. California files a civil lawsuit against Exxon Mobil, Shell, Chevron, ConocoPhillips, and BP, alleging deception and downplaying fossil fuel risks.

2. The lawsuit seeks accountability from the companies and the creation of a fund to finance recovery efforts after storms and fires.

3. Allegations include false advertising, damage to natural resources, and unlawful business practices related to climate change deception.

United Auto Workers go on strike

-CNN

1. Former President Barack Obama calls for auto companies to prioritize fair treatment of workers and honor their sacrifices during the industry's previous struggles.

2. Stellantis offers a 20% increase in the top wage scale, similar to offers from GM and Ford, but the union rejects all offers for falling short of their demands.

3. Ford and GM confirm 2,600 UAW members will be laid off due to the strike, with potential ripple effects on production at other plants.

Two Vegas casinos fell victim to cyberattacks, shattering the image of impenetrable casino security

-East Idaho News

1. MGM Resorts International has experienced a cybersecurity breach, resulting in unauthorized charges to customers' credit cards and disrupted casino and hotel operations.

2. The breach has raised concerns about the security measures in place at casinos, as hackers often exploit human-related weaknesses such as phishing attacks.

3. Caesars Entertainment, another major casino owner, has also confirmed being hit by a cybersecurity attack, potentially putting the personal information of millions of customers at risk. There are concerns that more attacks could occur if a ransom was paid to regain control of the data.

🤖 TECH NEWS

iPhone 15 vs Z Flip 5 vs Z Fold 5: Comparing specs, price, cameras

-Mashable

1. The iPhone 15, Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 5, and Z Flip 5 are compared based on their specs, cameras, and value.

2. The Samsung phones have superior displays with 120Hz refresh rates, while the iPhone 15 has a 60Hz refresh rate display.

3. The Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 5 wins in terms of value, while the iPhone 15 is the cheapest option starting at $800.

Apple witnesses strong demand for iPhone 15 series in China, but challenges persist amid competition from Chinese rivals

-Global Times

1. The newly released iPhone 15 series faces increased competition in the Chinese market.

2. Chinese netizens have mixed reactions to the newly released iPhone models.

3. Apple showcased its next-generation iPhone 15 lineup and other updates during the event.

FDA appears to back Apple in iPhone 12 fracas as France halts sales due to radiation fears

-New York Post

1. The FDA is not specifically investigating the safety of the iPhone 12, contrary to earlier reports. They state that there is no scientific evidence linking cell phone radiation to health problems below the exposure limits set by the FCC.

2. French scientists flagged the iPhone 12 for emitting levels of electromagnetic radiation that slightly exceed European Union restrictions, but still below the level that poses a health risk. Apple disputes these claims and states that the device is compliant with global standards.

3. Despite potential bans from other EU countries, the FDA does not indicate any plans to ban the iPhone 12 in the United States. The World Health Organization has not substantiated any adverse health effects caused by cell phone radiation.
