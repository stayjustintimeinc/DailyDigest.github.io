FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day, January 13th, in 1128, the Order of the Knights Templar was officially established. Fueled by their devotion to protect Christian pilgrims traveling to the Holy Land, the Templars grew in power and wealth throughout Europe during the Middle Ages. They developed innovative financial systems, established an extensive network of fortifications, and even influenced early banking practices. However, their influence eventually led to their downfall, as King Philip IV of France, indebted to the order, orchestrated their downfall, leading to their dissolution in 1312.

Live updates: US strikes on Houthis, Israel-Hamas war, Gaza crisis

-CNN

1. At least 82 journalists have been killed since the start of the Israel-Hamas conflict, making it the deadliest period for journalists since 1992.

2. The head of the UN agency working in Gaza warns of a looming famine and urges leaders to enable the delivery of essential goods and a humanitarian ceasefire.

Two Navy Seals missing after harsh waves knock them overboard off the coast of Somalia

-New York Post

1. Two Navy Seals are missing after falling into the water off the coast of Somalia during a nighttime boarding attempt on a vessel in the Gulf of Aden.

2. The Seals were on an interdiction mission intercepting weapons bound for Houthi-controlled Yemen.

3. The search and rescue efforts are ongoing, and more information about the mission has not been released by US Central Command.

Israel strikes southern Gaza city swollen with displaced people

-BBC.com

1. Ten people, including two displaced families, were killed in Israeli strikes in Rafah, Gaza.

2. The population in Rafah has increased as displaced people from other parts of Gaza seek shelter there.

3. The healthcare system in Rafah is struggling to meet the needs of the influx of displaced Palestinians and their families.

Quaker Oats Recalls More Products Over Potential Salmonella Contamination

-The New York Times

1. Quaker Oats Company has expanded its recall of products due to possible salmonella contamination.

2. The initial recall included 43 products, and 24 more products were added to the list this week.

3. Customers are advised to check their pantries for the recalled products and dispose of them, as salmonella can cause serious infections.

Consumer Reports takes EVs for a spin with updated tests and ratings

-NPR

1. Consumer Reports, a nonprofit organization that evaluates new cars, is adjusting its testing methods and criteria to accommodate the shift towards electric vehicles (EVs). As the auto industry moves towards battery-powered vehicles, Consumer Reports has been adding EV-specific tests and evaluations to capture the unique aspects of these vehicles.

2. The organization now evaluates factors such as the ease of plugging and unplugging a vehicle, the functionality of in-vehicle apps for locating charging stations, and the actual driving range of EVs on a single charge. Consumer Reports conducts extensive tests where cars are driven from full charge to empty, towing them back to the track if necessary.

3. Consumer Reports' surveys indicate that EVs currently have 79% more problems than gas-powered cars. Established automakers tend to face issues related to electric components, such as motors, batteries, and software, while newer automakers, like Rivian and Lucid, face fewer problems due to their emphasis on electric technology from the start.
