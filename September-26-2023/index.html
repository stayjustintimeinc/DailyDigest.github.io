FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

Fun Fact: On this day, September 26th, in 1983, Stanislav Petrov, a Soviet military officer, single-handedly prevented a nuclear war by saving the world from a potential cataclysmic disaster. As an early warning system detected an incoming missile strike from the United States, Petrov's prompt decision to disregard the warnings as a false alarm avoided a devastating nuclear retaliation, offering a much-needed lesson in the perils of technological overload and the importance of human judgment in critical situations. This incident is known as "The Man Who Saved the World" and highlights the significance of human intuition amidst advancing technology.

🌎 WORLD NEWS

Canada Parliament Speaker Resigns Amid Row Over Praising Nazi Veteran

-NDTV

1. Anthony Rota, the speaker of Canada's parliament, resigned after publicly celebrating a Ukrainian veteran with ties to a Nazi-linked military unit.

2. Rota apologized for his error and expressed regret for the pain caused to Jewish communities in Canada and worldwide.

3. This controversy has compromised all 338 Members of Parliament and has given a propaganda victory to Russia, which accuses the Ukrainian government of espousing Nazi ideals.

Ukraine Acknowledges Doubt After Russia Shows Video of Naval Commander 'Allegedly Alive'

-The New York Times

1. Ukraine is "clarifying" the results of a strike on a Russian naval headquarters that allegedly killed the commander of Russia's Black Sea Fleet and 33 other officers.

2. Russian state media released footage of the admiral, Viktor Sokolov, in a meeting with defense officials, raising doubts about his reported death.

3. The authenticity of the video and the outcome of the strike are still uncertain, and Ukraine claims that the admiral is among the dead.

Russian appeals court upholds opposition leader Alexei Navalny's 19-year prison term

-NPR

1. Russian opposition leader Alexei Navalny's 19-year prison sentence has been upheld by a Moscow appeals court.

2. Navalny was convicted on extremism-related charges in August and is already serving a 9-year sentence on separate charges.

3. Navalny's supporters were not allowed to attend the closed hearing of his appeal, as authorities feared "provocations."

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

Target closing 9 stores citing violence, theft

-The Washington Post

1. Target is closing down nine stores in urban areas across four states due to increased violence related to theft and organized retail crime. The affected cities include Portland, Seattle, New York, and the San Francisco-Oakland area.

2. The company has experienced a significant increase in theft incidents involving violence or threats of violence, with losses due to shrink amounting to $500 million.

3. Other retailers, such as Home Depot, Lowe's, Dollar Tree, Dick's Sporting Goods, and Ulta, have also reported concerns about theft and organized retail crime, leading to closures of their own stores.

US government and 17 states sue Amazon in landmark monopoly case

-CNN

1. The US government and 17 states have filed a landmark lawsuit against Amazon, accusing the e-commerce giant of monopoly behavior and unfair competition.

2. The 172-page complaint alleges that Amazon promotes its own services at the expense of third-party sellers and forces sellers to offer their products at the lowest prices on its platform.

3. The lawsuit seeks a court order to block Amazon from engaging in alleged anticompetitive behavior, and there is a possibility of a potential breakup of the company.

JPMorgan's Jamie Dimon says interest rates 'may go up more,' says he hopes for soft landing

-Fox Business

1. JPMorgan Chase CEO Jamie Dimon stated that U.S. interest rates "may go up more" but hopes for a soft landing.

2. Dimon highlighted potential risks such as Ukraine, oil, gas, war, and Europe, while cautioning about the limits of monetary and fiscal stimulus.

3. He warned that a 7% interest rate could cause stress in the system and advised clients to be prepared for that possibility.

iPhone 15 Pro Max zooms to second place in DxOMark mobile camera rankings

-Notebookcheck.net

1. The iPhone 15 Pro Max ranks #2 in DxOMark's camera rankings with a score of 154 points.

3. However, the 15 Pro Max couldn't beat the P60 Pro, which still holds the top spot with 156 points. It also faced challenges with zoom consistency and unwanted artifacts in low-light shots.
