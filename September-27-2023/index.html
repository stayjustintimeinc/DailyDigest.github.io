FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day, September 27th, in 1825, the world's first public railway opened between Stockton and Darlington in England, showcasing the potential for locomotive transportation. The inaugural train journey, which was led by the steam locomotive named "Locomotion No. 1," transported approximately 450 passengers and 90 tons of coal. The event marked a significant milestone in the development of railways and paved the way for the widespread adoption of this revolutionary mode of transportation worldwide.

Saudi tourism minister acknowledges historic Israeli presence at Riyadh confab

-The Times of Israel

1. Israeli Tourism Minister Haim Katz visited Riyadh in an unprecedented trip to Saudi Arabia as part of the growing rapprochement between the two countries.

2. Katz held meetings with various foreign officials during his visit, including counterparts from Monaco, Malta, Albania, Greece, and representatives from Kyrgyzstan and Bahrain.

3. Israel and Saudi Arabia are moving closer to a normalization deal, with the US actively engaging both countries in brokering an agreement. Saudi Arabia also seeks a defense pact with the US and Israeli concessions to the Palestinians as part of the framework.

Iraq fire: Around 100 killed in blaze at wedding party in Qaraqosh

-BBC

1. At least 94 people have died and 100 others are injured in a fire that broke out during a wedding in Iraq's biggest Christian town.

2. The fire was sparked by fireworks set off during the bride and groom's dance, and it was fueled by highly flammable metal and plastic composite panels.

3. The groom and bride survived, contrary to initial reports, and an investigation is underway to hold those responsible accountable for the lack of safety measures.

Russia accuses Ukraine's Western allies of helping attack its Black Sea Fleet headquarters

-Yahoo News

1. Russia accused Ukraine's Western allies of planning and conducting the missile strike on the Black Sea Fleet's headquarters on the annexed Crimean Peninsula. Moscow claimed that the attack was implemented with the assistance of American and British security agencies and in coordination with them, using Western intelligence means, NATO satellite assets, and reconnaissance planes.

2. Unconfirmed news reports suggested that Storm Shadow missiles provided by the UK and France were used in the attack. The UK Ministry of Defense did not comment on this matter.

3. Ukraine claimed that the strike wounded 105 people and alleged that the fleet's commander, Adm. Viktor Sokolov, was among the casualties. However, video footage released by the Russian Defense Ministry showed Sokolov participating in a video conference, and later, he was seen speaking to journalists, denying the claims of his death.

In latest recall, Hyundai and Kia ask owners of 3.3 million vehicles to park outside due to risk of fire

-CNN

1. The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration has announced a recall for about 3.3 million Hyundai and Kia vehicles due to a risk of sudden fires.

2. The recall is the latest in a series of fire-related recalls for Hyundai and Kia vehicles in recent years.

3. Owners of the affected vehicles are advised to bring their vehicles to a dealership for a fuse replacement and to watch out for signs of overheating or fire.

UAW again threatens to expand strikes at Detroit automakers if progress isn't made by Friday

-CNBC

1. The United Auto Workers union will announce expanded strikes at General Motors, Ford Motor, and Stellantis plants if significant progress is not made in negotiations by 10 a.m. ET on Friday.

2. The strikes currently involve around 18,300 workers, or 12.5% of the UAW's 146,000 members, with labor contracts expiring on September 14.

3. The automakers have made record contract offers with approximately 20% hourly wage increases, bonuses, and improved benefits, but the union is still planning expanded strikes.

Lina Khan vs. Jeff Bezos: This Is Big Tech's Real Cage Match

-The New York Times

1. The chair of the Federal Trade Commission, Lina Khan, filed a lawsuit against Amazon, accusing the company of being a monopolist and using unfair tactics.

2. Lina Khan's arguments could potentially lead to major changes in the competitive landscape for tech companies.

3. Lina Khan's confrontation with Jeff Bezos, Amazon's executive chair, in court could have implications beyond Amazon's employees and customers.
