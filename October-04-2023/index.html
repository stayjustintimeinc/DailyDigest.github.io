A bus in Venice plunged 50 feet, killing 21 in a fiery crash

-NPR

1. A bus carrying foreign tourists in Venice, Italy, crashed, killing 21 people and injuring at least 15 others.

2. The victims included tourists from Ukraine, Germany, and other countries, including children.

3. The cause of the accident is under investigation, and the age of the overpass and the fact that the bus was electric contributed to the severity of the crash.

Pakistan orders Afghan asylum seekers out of country by November

-BBC.com

1. Pakistan has ordered 1.7 million unauthorized Afghan asylum seekers to leave the country by November due to a surge in attacks along the border, which Islamabad blames on Afghanistan-based operatives.

2. The Taliban government has urged Pakistan to reconsider its order, denying providing refuge for militants targeting Pakistan.

3. Pakistan's crackdown on "illegal" Afghans includes a taskforce to identify and confiscate private businesses and assets, and tighter restrictions on Afghans entering the country from November.

Former Russian state TV journalist gets 8 1/2-year sentence in absentia for Ukraine war criticism

-ABC News

1. Marina Ovsyannikova, a former state TV journalist, has been sentenced to 8 1/2 years in prison in absentia for protesting Russia's war in Ukraine.

2. Ovsyannikova was charged with spreading false information about the Russian army and staged a protest near the Kremlin. She escaped to France with her daughter but was prosecuted and tried in absentia.

3. The crackdown on dissent in Russia has intensified since the invasion of Ukraine, with thousands of people facing charges and persecution for speaking out against the war. Independent media and opposition figures have been targeted.
