🌎 WORLD NEWS

Deadly quake strikes Nepal, toppling homes and killing at least 157 people

-CNN

1. At least 157 people have been killed in a 5.6 magnitude earthquake in northwestern Nepal, with the death toll expected to rise.

2. The quake caused multiple buildings to collapse and affected areas as far as India's capital, New Delhi.

3. Rescue and search operations are underway, with some villages reporting that up to 90% of houses have collapsed.

Israel strikes Hezbollah targets in tense exchange on Lebanon border

-The Hill

1. Israeli military exchanges fire with Hezbollah militants at the Lebanon-Israel border.

2. Israel launches airstrikes on Hezbollah positions, while the group retaliates with rocket attacks.

3. Skirmishes between Hezbollah and Israel escalate as Israel continues its ground invasion of Gaza.

Former Guinea dictator Moussa Dadis Camara recaptured after armed prison escape

-The Guardian

1. Former junta leader, Moussa Dadis Camara, has been recaptured and returned to prison after an apparent attempted jailbreak led by a heavily armed commando.

2. At least two other former officials on trial for a 2009 massacre during Camara's presidency were also taken during the earlier operation.

3. It is unclear whether Camara escaped willingly or if he was kidnapped, but his lawyer believes his life is in danger.

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

Warren Buffett sits tight on cash as Berskhire investments take a hit: reports

-Fox Business

1. Berkshire Hathaway, led by Warren Buffett, reported losses of $12.8 billion in the third quarter, significantly higher than the previous year's loss of $2.8 billion.

2. Despite the losses, the company's portfolio is still valued at over $341 billion, with Apple accounting for nearly half of its value.

3. Berkshire Hathaway's success is measured by its operating earnings, which saw a 41% surge, reaching $10.8 billion.

Sam Bankman-Fried's shadow still looms over the crypto industry

-The Verge

1. Sam Bankman-Fried, founder of FTX, has been found guilty on seven counts including charges of wire fraud, revealing FTX as a fraudulent operation from the start.

2. The collapse of FTX highlights the need for greater scrutiny and regulation within the cryptocurrency industry.

3. The industry is now working to repair its reputation and focus on the fundamental promise of decentralization, emphasizing that Bankman-Fried's crimes do not represent the entire crypto world.

How to get a mortgage as low as 4.8% right now

-Business Insider

1. Sales of existing homes in the US declined 15.4% from a year ago due to high mortgage rates.

2. Buying a new-construction home may offer a glimmer of hope for lower mortgage rates, potentially as low as 4.8%.

3. Builders may offer incentives such as lower interest rates or financing assistance to sell more houses, and buyers should negotiate for every available incentive to save money.

🤖 TECH NEWS

Apple Argued Safari Is Three Different Browsers to Avoid Regulation

-MacRumors

1. Apple claimed that Safari is three distinct web browsers in an attempt to evade regulation in the European Union. They argued that Safari for iOS, iPadOS, and macOS serve different purposes and have different features.

2. The European Commission rejected Apple's claim, stating that Safari is a single web browser regardless of the device it is accessed on. As a result, Apple must ensure that Safari adheres to the Digital Markets Act's requirements.

3. Non-compliance with the regulations can lead to EU investigations, substantial fines, and the imposition of behavioral or structural remedies. There are concerns about whether Apple's argument violates the anti-circumvention provision of the DMA.

S Pen Slot Finally Coming to Galaxy Z Fold?

-Droid Life

1. Samsung is working on a patent to incorporate a built-in S Pen in the Galaxy Z Fold series.

2. The patent showcases different designs with slots for the S Pen, either on the side or back of the device.

3. The challenge for Samsung is to keep the device slim while adding the S Pen, but it is uncertain if this design will be implemented in the upcoming Galaxy Z Fold 6.

I’m scared of next-gen Nvidia GPUs, and you should be too
