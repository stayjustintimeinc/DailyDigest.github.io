In landmark ruling, Israel's Supreme Court rejects right-wing changes to judiciary

-NPR

1. Israel's Supreme Court has overturned a law proposed by Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's government that aimed to transform the country's judiciary system, following months of protests by Israelis.

2. The court ruled by a narrow 8-7 majority that the change to the judiciary would have caused "severe and unprecedented harm to the core characteristics of Israel as a democratic state."

3. The decision further complicates Netanyahu's tenure as Prime Minister, as his right-wing political partners had suggested they might challenge the court's legitimacy if it overturned the law.

Powerful Earthquake Hits Japan, Causing Tsunami and Killing Six

-The Wall Street Journal

1. A powerful 7.6-magnitude earthquake struck the west coast of Japan, causing a tsunami wave of at least 4 feet, as well as home collapses and a large fire.

2. Six people were killed in the earthquake, and authorities were responding to at least six cases of people trapped inside collapsed homes.

3. The earthquake occurred in Ishikawa prefecture, and the government and police are working to assess the damage and provide assistance to affected areas.

Muhammad Yunus: Nobel Peace Prize winner sentenced to 6 months in jail

-The Associated Press

1. Nobel Peace Prize winner Muhammad Yunus has been sentenced to six months in jail for violating labor laws in Bangladesh.

2. The case revolves around Grameen Telecom, which Yunus founded, and alleged violations including failure to make workers permanent employees and not distributing dividends to staff.

3. Yunus plans to appeal the verdict, while his supporters believe he is being targeted due to strained relations with Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina.

Chinese factory activity slows in December in a 3rd straight month of contraction

-ABC News

1. The official purchasing managers index (PMI) for manufacturing in China fell to 49 in December, indicating contraction for the third consecutive month. This suggests weak demand and reflects the sluggishness of the world's second-largest economy.

2. China's economy had shown signs of improvement in November, with growth in factory output and retail sales. The government has implemented measures like increased construction spending and eased restrictions on home-buying to stimulate domestic demand.

3. While the construction industry is thriving, driven by infrastructure projects, the service sector PMI remained unchanged at 49.3. The global demand for manufactured goods continues to be impacted, affecting supply chains across the region.

Over 657,000 infant formula cans recalled over potential bacteria contamination

-Good Morning America

1. Over 675,000 cans of Nutramigen Hypoallergenic Infant Formula Powder are being recalled due to possible bacterial contamination.

2. The recall is a precautionary measure to ensure the safety of infants, as the bacteria can be dangerous for babies under 2 months, premature babies, and babies with weakened immune systems.

3. Reckitt and Mead Johnson Nutrition have not received any reports of illnesses related to the recalled formula cans.
