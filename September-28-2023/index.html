FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day, September 28th, in 1928, Alexander Fleming discovered penicillin by accident. While working with bacteria cultures in his laboratory at St. Mary's Hospital in London, Fleming found that a mold called Penicillium notatum had contaminated one of his petri dishes. To his surprise, the bacteria near the mold did not grow, leading him to the realization that the mold secreted a substance capable of killing bacteria. This accidental discovery laid the foundation for the development of the world's first antibiotic, revolutionizing medical treatments and saving countless lives since then.

🌎 WORLD NEWS

Teen arrested after Sycamore Gap tree cut down, police say

-The Washington Post

1. The iconic Sycamore Gap tree in Northumberland, England, has been deliberately vandalized and is now just a stump.

2. Authorities have arrested a 16-year-old boy as a suspect in the incident, but the investigation is still ongoing.

3. The fallen tree had significant cultural and historical importance, being featured in films and considered a beloved symbol in the local community.

Student gunman kills three in Rotterdam university shooting

-Reuters

1. A gunman killed three people in Rotterdam, Netherlands, targeting a local woman, her daughter, and a male teacher at a university hospital.

2. The suspect, a 32-year-old university student, had a prior criminal record relating to animal abuse.

3. The motive behind the shootings remains unknown, and there is no evidence of a second shooter.

UK on track for record tax levels, IFS think tank says

-BBC

1. The government is projected to oversee the largest tax-raising parliament since records began, with taxes expected to reach 37% of national income by the next general election in 2024.

2. The Institute for Fiscal Studies (IFS) forecasts that the government will collect over £100bn more in tax next year compared to pre-2019 levels.

3. The tax rate increase is not solely due to the pandemic but also reflects decisions to increase government spending, demographic change, healthcare pressures, and some reversal of austerity measures.

China Evergrande Suspends Trading as New Trouble Roils Property Market

-The New York Times

1. China Evergrande, the world's most debt-saddled real estate developer, faces a stream of bad news, including the detention of staff at its wealth management arm and two former top executives, and its billionaire chairman being under police surveillance.

2. Investors have sold off their shares, causing Evergrande's stock to plummet more than 40% in a week. The company has also suspended trading in the stock of its three publicly traded companies in Hong Kong.

3. Evergrande's troubles deepen amid an ongoing investigation by the authorities for suspicion of "illegal crimes", raising concerns about the company's ability to resolve its debt restructuring and the impact on China's real estate landscape.
