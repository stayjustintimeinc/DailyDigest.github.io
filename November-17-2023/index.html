FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day in history, November 17th, 1970, the world's first known hijacking of an aircraft for ransom occurred. A Boeing 727, bound for Miami from New York, was diverted to Cuba by a man named William Potts Jr. The hijacker demanded $1 million in ransom and threatened to blow up the plane with 86 passengers on board. After landing in Cuba, Potts surrendered to authorities and was later imprisoned.

What animal has the unique ability to sleep with one half of its brain while the other half remains awake?

Dolphins have this extraordinary ability. They can let one half of their brain sleep while the other half remains alert. This adaptation is known as unihemispheric slow-wave sleep. It allows them to be partly alert to potential predators and enables them to come to the surface for air, a critical necessity for these marine mammals. This sleeping method is also observed in some birds and is a fascinating example of evolutionary adaptation.
