FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day, December 11th, in 1936, King Edward VIII of the United Kingdom officially abdicated the throne after a reign of only 325 days. His decision was driven by his desire to marry American divorcee Wallis Simpson, which was deemed unacceptable by the British government and the Church of England at the time. Edward VIII's abdication led to his younger brother, Albert, assuming the throne as King George VI.

Jailed Russian opposition figure Alexei Navalny is missing, supporters say

-The Washington Post

1. Supporters of Alexei Navalny have lost contact with him and are unable to ascertain his whereabouts for almost a week.

2. Navalny was no longer in the prison colony where he had been held and his lawyers and family were not informed of his new location.

3. Concerns are raised about Navalny's health as he recently became ill in his cell, further increasing alarm about his disappearance.

In first speech, Argentina's Javier Milei warns nation of painful economic shock

-NPR

1. Argentina's newly sworn-in President, Javier Milei, presented figures highlighting the nation's economic emergency and emphasized the need for immediate action.

2. The country is facing significant challenges, including 143% annual inflation, a plunging currency, high levels of poverty, a fiscal deficit, a trade deficit, and a large debt owed to the International Monetary Fund.

3. President Milei stated that the adjustment measures would primarily affect the state rather than the private sector, with the aim of laying the foundation for future growth and prosperity. However, he is likely to face opposition from the Peronist movement and unions.

How much trouble is PM in over Rwanda bill?

-BBC.com

1. The government is in deeper peril than previously believed, with potential rebel MPs hardening their resolve against the emergency Rwanda legislation.

2. Some MPs who were previously inclined to support the government now have doubts about amending the bill at a later stage.

3. The One Nation group has threatened to revolt if the bill moves further towards the right, and Prime Minister Sunak may withdraw the bill if faced with significant rebellion.

Macy's mulls $5.8 billion buyout offer, as stock surges after the news

-Yahoo Finance

1. Macy's has received a $5.8 billion buyout offer from real estate investor Arkhouse Management and Brigade Capital Management.

2. The offer values Macy's at about $21 a share, representing a 32.4% premium to Macy's closing price on Nov. 30.

3. Macy's board is considering the offer, but has to weigh the value of the company's real estate holdings and potential disruption to the business during the holiday shopping season.

Betting grows that S&P 500 will hit record high, with Oppenheimer joining Wall Street's bullish calls for 2024

-MarketWatch

1. Strategists at Oppenheimer Asset Management predict that the S&P 500 index will reach 5,200 by the end of 2024, implying a 13% gain from its current level.

2. They expect the Federal Reserve to lower interest rates by the second half of 2024, contrary to the market's expectation of a rate cut in the first quarter.

3. Oppenheimer's strategists believe that cyclical sectors like technology, communications services, and consumer-discretionary will continue to perform well in 2024, with corporate revenues and earnings growing over the course of the year.

Oracle shares slide as revenue misses estimates

-CNBC

1. Oracle reported a 5% year-over-year growth in revenue for the fiscal second quarter, falling short of Wall Street expectations.

2. Net income increased by 44% to $2.5 billion or 89 cents per share, compared to $1.74 billion or 63 cents per share a year ago.

3. Oracle's revenue from cloud services and license support totaled $9.64 billion, a 12% increase, while revenue from cloud and on-premises licenses fell 18% to $1.18 billion.
