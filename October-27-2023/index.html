FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day in history, October 27th, in 1904, the New York City Subway system officially opened to the public. It was the first-ever underground rapid transit system in the United States, consisting of a 9.1-mile (14.6 km) track that connected City Hall in Manhattan to 145th Street and Broadway in the Bronx. Passengers were thrilled by the technological marvel as they experienced the smooth and efficient transportation offered by electric trains running below the bustling city streets. The subway quickly became an incredible success, carrying over 150,000 commuters on its very first day of operation. Since that inaugural day, the New York City Subway has grown exponentially, expanding to become one of the most extensive and heavily used subway systems in the world, with 472 stations and over 28 miles (44.8 km) of track. So, if you're ever in New York City, take a ride on the iconic subway and commemorate its historical opening on this very day, October 27th!

US strikes 2 facilities linked to Iranian-backed militias in Syria following series of attacks on US forces in Middle East

-CNN

1. The US carried out airstrikes targeting two facilities linked to Iranian-backed militias in eastern Syria, in response to attacks against US forces in the region.

2. The strikes were aimed at a weapons and ammunition storage facility in Abu Kamal near the Syria-Iraq border, and were described as "narrowly tailored in self-defense" by Defense Secretary Lloyd Austin.

3. The US holds Iran responsible for the attacks on its forces and has promised further strikes if necessary, while also deploying more troops and military assets to the Middle East.

Israel conducts 2nd ground raid in Gaza as war with Hamas threatens to grow and civilian death toll soars

-CBS News

1. Israeli forces conducted a ground raid into Gaza for the second consecutive night, striking dozens of targets on the outskirts of Gaza City.

2. The raid resulted in no Israeli casualties, but one rocket fired from Gaza hit Tel Aviv, causing significant destruction and injuring three people.

3. The death toll in Gaza has been soaring, with disagreements between Israel and Hamas on the exact number of casualties, and concerns about potential war crimes being committed by both sides.

-Yahoo Finance

1. Ford Motor Company missed earnings expectations after reaching a tentative agreement with the United Auto Workers union, causing uncertainty around the financial impact of the recent strikes.
