🌎 WORLD NEWS

Nobel Peace Prize: Narges Mohammadi, Jailed Iranian Activist, Is 2023 Laureate

-The New York Times

1. Narges Mohammadi, an Iranian activist serving a 10-year prison sentence, has been awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for her fight against the oppression of women in Iran and her promotion of human rights and freedom.

2. The award also recognizes the hundreds of thousands of people who protested against the discriminatory and oppressive policies of Iran's theocratic regime, particularly targeting women.

3. Despite her continued imprisonment, Mohammadi remains committed to her activism and fighting against gender-based oppression until the liberation of women.

Death toll from Indian Himalayan flash floods rises to 42, scores still missing

-Reuters India

1. At least 42 people have been killed in flash floods in the Indian Himalayas caused by a burst glacial lake.

2. The flooding has impacted the lives of 22,000 people in the state of Sikkim, with 142 people still reported missing.

3. Efforts are ongoing to rescue stranded tourists and establish an early warning system for glacial floods in the region.

American tourist arrested for smashing Israel Museum statues

-BBC.com

1. An American tourist has been arrested for smashing ancient Roman statues at the Israel Museum in Jerusalem.

2. The tourist claimed that the statues were "against the Torah" and represented idol worship.

3. The damaged statues, a sculpture of Athena's head and a statue of a griffin, have been moved for restoration.

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

Getting a $7500 tax credit for an electric car will soon get a lot easier

-NPR

1. Starting in January, electric vehicle (EV) buyers will be able to receive the federal tax credit of up to $7,500 as cash on the day of purchase, instead of having to wait until tax season.

2. The new system allows dealerships to register with the IRS and confirm the eligibility of a vehicle for the tax credit by using the vehicle identification number, eliminating the need for buyers to navigate complex rules and requirements.

3. The credit can be obtained regardless of the buyer's tax bill, meaning low- and middle-income families who owe less than $7,500 in taxes will still be eligible to receive the full benefit of the credit.

Treasury yields are climbing: 'There's never really been such an attractive opportunity for fixed-income investments'

-MarketWatch

1. Financial experts suggest increasing exposure to longer-term Treasurys and other fixed income investments as a way to navigate the volatility caused by rising bond yields.

2. High-yield savings accounts, certificates of deposit, and money market-mutual funds are considered attractive options for earning rewards on parked cash.

3. Advisers recommend a "barbell" approach that combines longer-term Treasurys with other fixed income investments to manage risks and take advantage of current attractive opportunities in the fixed income market.

The housing market is following a similar playbook from the 1980s when mortgage rates doubled. Here's what it could mean for homebuyers today.

-Yahoo Finance

1. Bank of America highlights that the current US housing market is reminiscent of the 1980s, with similarities including high inflation, surging mortgage rates, and pent-up demand from a growing demographic.

2. While prices are unlikely to rise further, they may slightly fall but not to the extent seen during the 2008 housing crash.

3. Persistently high mortgage rates may make purchasing a home more challenging for many millennials, despite favorable demographics. Limited inventories, high prices, and labor shortages could also pose headwinds in the housing market.

Samsung Galaxy S23 Ultra camera battle with iPhone 15 Pro Max

-9to5Mac
