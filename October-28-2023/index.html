FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day in history, October 28th, in 1886, the Statue of Liberty was dedicated in New York City. A colossal neoclassical sculpture, standing at 305 feet (93 meters) tall from the base to the tip of its torch, the Statue of Liberty was a gift from the people of France to the United States. It quickly became an icon representing freedom, democracy, and the spirit of hope for millions of immigrants arriving in America. Today, it stands as a well-recognized symbol of the United States.

Pope says it's "urgent" to guarantee governance roles for women during meeting on church future

-CBS News

1. The gathering of Catholic bishops and laypeople called for greater participation of women in church governance positions.

2. Research on allowing women to be deacons is to be released within a year.

3. The synod's final text did not mention homosexuality, but acknowledged the need for inclusivity and understanding for marginalized individuals.

Mexico hurricane death toll hits 39; president slams critics

-Reuters

1. The death toll from Hurricane Otis in Acapulco has risen to 39 according to the Mexican government.

2. President Andres Manuel Lopez Obrador accused his opponents of exaggerating the extent of the disaster for political gain.

3. Looting and insufficient government aid have been reported in Acapulco, with concerns that the number of fatalities could increase.

Netanyahu says the Gaza war has entered a new stage and will be 'long and difficult'

-The Associated Press

1. Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu announces a "second stage" in the war against Hamas, involving ground forces entering Gaza and intensified attacks from air and sea.

2. The bombardment by Israel causes severe damage to Gaza's infrastructure, cutting off communications and isolating the population.

3. Netanyahu expresses determination to secure the release of Israeli hostages held by Hamas and to destroy the military and governmental capabilities of the group.

UAW reaches tentative agreement with Stellantis, expands strike at GM

-CNN

1. The United Auto Workers union reached a tentative agreement with Stellantis, resulting in the end of the strike for 14,600 workers and the reopening of a closed Illinois assembly plant.

2. However, the strike against General Motors (GM) expanded as nearly 4,000 GM workers joined the strike after the tentative agreement with Stellantis was announced.

3. The UAW National Stellantis Council will vote on the agreement, and it still needs to be ratified by rank-and-file members before going into effect. The details of the agreement are not yet known, but it is expected to include pay raises and a cost-of-living adjustment.

The Crypto Whistleblower at the Center of the Sam Bankman-Fried Storm

-Rolling Stone

1. Tiffany Fong, a reluctant crypto content creator, has gained access to FTX founder Sam Bankman-Fried during his trial for fraud charges and has spent significant time with him at his childhood home.

2. Fong's access to Bankman-Fried is only rivalled by author Michael Lewis, who spent hundreds of hours with him for a book about his rise and fall in the crypto industry.

3. Fong, who grew up in Las Vegas and had a career crisis before backpacking around the world, gained fame in the crypto community for her "Celsius leaks" last year.

MRI machine traps nurse in freak accident

-Fox News

1. A nurse at a medical center in California was pinned between an MRI machine and a bed during a freak accident.

2. The nurse suffered "crushing injuries" and required surgery after the MRI machine's magnetic force suddenly pulled a hospital bed towards it.

3. The investigation found that the medical center failed to provide radiologic services in a safe manner and breached several MRI safety policies.

Google Maps is rolling out AI-powered features for planning trips on iPhone

-AppleInsider

1. Google Maps is introducing AI features for iOS users to simplify trip planning and provide detailed, visual turn-by-turn directions.

2. The Immersive View feature allows users to preview their entire route in a multidimensional view and plan their departure based on simulated traffic and weather conditions.

3. Google Maps is also improving users' comprehension of their surroundings with the Lens in Maps feature, which utilizes AI and augmented reality to provide details about nearby amenities and landmarks.

-MacRumors

1. Apple has released the first betas of iOS 17.2 and iPadOS 17.2 to public beta testers, following the release of the developer betas.

2. New features in iOS 17.2 include the Journal app, collaborative playlists in Apple Music, stickers as tapback reactions in Messages, and more.

3. Apple has also released new public betas of tvOS 17.2 and watchOS 10.2, while a new 24-inch iMac and an AR/VR headset are reportedly in development.

Use this hidden Google feature to detect and delete your personal information in search

-CNBC

1. Google has a hidden feature that alerts users when it finds their personal information in search results.

2. This feature proactively searches for phone numbers, email addresses, and home addresses.

3. While Google doesn't remove the information from the internet, it removes it from Google Search to make it harder for people to find.
