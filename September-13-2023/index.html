🌎 WORLD NEWS

Libya floods: Derna death toll rises as bodies wash ashore, aid trickles in

-NBC News

1. The city of Derna in eastern Libya is facing a devastating flood crisis, with thousands of deaths and missing people.

2. The floods were caused by two dams bursting on the Wadi Derna River during a storm, leading to waves 23 feet high rushing through the city.

3. Rescue operations are ongoing, but the window of opportunity to find survivors is closing. International aid is needed for search-and-rescue efforts and to provide support to the affected population.

Lee prompts hurricane and storm surge watches in parts of New England; new depression expected in Atlantic

-South Florida Sun Sentinel

1. Hurricane Lee is expected to impact the coast of New England with a hurricane watch for northern Maine and a tropical storm watch for coastal New England.

2. Storm surge is also a concern, with a surge watch for Cape Cod and Nantucket predicted to be between 2 and 4 feet.

3. Lee has weakened to a Category 2 storm with sustained wind speeds of 105 mph, but is expected to grow and bring potential flooding to eastern New England, New Brunswick, and Nova Scotia.

A Mexican ufologist claims to show 2 alien corpses to Congress

-NPR

1. A self-described ufologist presented two allegedly nonhuman bodies in caskets during a hearing on extraterrestrials in Mexico's Congress.

2. The bodies, resembling stereotypical depictions of aliens, were found in Peru in 2017 and are estimated to be 1,000 years old.

3. Forensic experts claimed the alleged aliens had big brains, big eyes, and lacked teeth, suggesting they only drank and did not chew.

U.A.W. Prepares for Partial Strike Against Detroit Automakers on Friday

-The New York Times

1. The United Auto Workers (U.A.W.) leader, Shawn Fain, announced that members are prepared for a strike against Detroit automakers if negotiations remain unresolved.

2. The U.A.W. plans to implement a targeted strike strategy rather than an all-out strike, aiming to disrupt production while keeping a large portion of workers employed.

3. The union's demands include a 40% wage increase over four years, regular cost-of-living adjustments, pensions for all workers, improved retiree benefits, shorter work hours, and an end to the tiered wage system.
