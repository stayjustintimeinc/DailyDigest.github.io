'Miracle drug' euphoria: Experts warn widespread use of weight loss medicine faces major hurdles

-CNBC

1. Dr. Kavita Patel sees challenges in the adoption of new obesity drugs, stating that data supporting their use for conditions like Alzheimer's and alcohol addiction is underdeveloped compared to data on conditions like chronic kidney disease, sleep apnea, cardiovascular risks, and diabetes control.

2. Novo Nordisk's obesity drug, Ozempic, has shown promising results in treating chronic kidney disease in Type 2 diabetic patients, but it still needs to overcome hurdles such as cost, adherence, and prescriber rate before being approved for uses outside of diabetes management.

3. Novo Nordisk's stock has increased by 9.82% since the announcement of Ozempic's efficacy in treating chronic kidney disease, while Eli Lilly, a competitor in the obesity drug market, has seen a 5.16% increase in the same period.

Sam Bankman-Fried trial: Timeline of the biggest moments so far

-NBC News

1. The trial of Sam Bankman-Fried, the founder of the cryptocurrency exchange FTX, is ongoing. The government's case began with a commodities broker testifying about his investment and subsequent losses in FTX.

2. Bankman-Fried's defense argues that he did not defraud anyone and that FTX's failure is a common risk in startups. Bankman-Fried is facing federal charges and potential decades in jail.

3. Testimonies from a former colleague and co-founder of FTX revealed the awareness of missing funds and the use of customer deposits to pay off loans, leading to loss of faith in the company. Bankman-Fried appeared visibly upset during the testimonies.

How much do UAW workers make? A look at hourly wages across US states

-USA TODAY

1. Thousands of United Auto Workers Union members are entering their fifth week of striking against the Detroit Three automakers. This includes 8,700 workers at Ford's largest plant, risking a daily loss of approximately $30 million in profit for the company.

2. Hourly union workers at the Ford Kentucky Truck Plant are demanding a fair contract and better pay. The vehicles produced at this plant generate $25 billion in revenue per year.

3. Autoworkers are striking for better pay, as they believe their wages have stagnated despite the Detroit car companies' near-record profits and increased CEO compensation. The three companies collectively reported $21 billion in earnings in the first half of this year.

Microsoft to Phase Out NTLM in Favor of Kerberos for Stronger Authentication

-The Hacker News

1. Microsoft plans to eliminate NT LAN Manager (NTLM) in Windows 11 in order to strengthen the Kerberos authentication protocol and enhance security.

2. New features for Windows 11 include Initial and Pass Through Authentication Using Kerberos (IAKerb) and a local Key Distribution Center (KDC) for Kerberos.

3. NTLM, which relies on password hashing and is susceptible to relay attacks, will be disabled in the future, but will still be available as a fallback for compatibility purposes.

Mortal Kombat 1 Shares "First Look" At The Kombat Pack DLC Fighter Omni-Man

2. The Kombat Pack roster will also include Peacemaker, Homelander, Quan Chi, Ermac, and Takeda when it launches in Spring 2024.

3. Additional Kameo Fighters will also be added to the roster.

Adobe's new generative AI tool is a game-changer for video editing

-ZDNet

1. Adobe unveils Project Fast Fill at Adobe MAX, a generative AI tool for video editing.

2. Project Fast Fill allows users to remove or add objects and change backgrounds in videos with simple text prompts.

3. The tool automatically applies edits to all frames of the video, adjusting for lighting changes, shadows, and object movement.
