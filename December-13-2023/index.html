Today, clip-on ties are popular among young boys, people with physical disabilities, and those who prefer a hassle-free tie-wearing experience. 🌎 WORLD NEWS

Israel-Hamas war: Ambush kills 9 Israeli soldiers in Gaza

-The Associated Press

1. Palestinian militants carry out a deadly ambush on Israeli soldiers in a dense neighborhood, killing at least nine.

2. Despite Israel's intense bombardment, Hamas continues to pose stiff resistance, indicating that Israel is far from destroying the group.

3. The ongoing conflict has resulted in a devastating humanitarian crisis, with thousands of Palestinians killed, displaced, and living in dire conditions.

Ukraine faces a daunting winter after Zelensky can’t convince Republicans to move quickly on new aid

-CNN

1. President Biden pledges US support for Ukraine, but with a shift in language indicating that assistance is not guaranteed indefinitely.

2. Ukraine and US military officials recognize the need for a change in strategy to push back against Russia and secure a better negotiating position.

3. The US allows General Antonio Aguto to be in Ukraine for longer periods to advise Ukrainian forces, aiming to improve intelligence sharing and coordination.

Donald Tusk sworn in as Polish PM

-POLITICO Europe

1. Donald Tusk is sworn in as Poland's new prime minister, promising to uphold the country's constitution and repair strained relations with Brussels.

2. The strong voter turnout in the recent election was driven by concerns about compliance with the law and the constitution, prompting a change in leadership.

3. The new government's priorities include unlocking frozen funds, supporting Ukraine, and implementing reforms in the judicial system while facing potential challenges from President Duda's veto power. Both Tusk and Duda pledge to work together for the benefit of Poland.

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

Federal Reserve issues FOMC statement

-Federal Reserve

1. Recent indicators suggest that economic activity has slowed and inflation remains elevated.

2. The Federal Reserve has decided to maintain the target range for the federal funds rate.

3. The Committee will continue to assess information and adjust policy as necessary to achieve its goals of maximum employment and 2 percent inflation.

Dow rallies more than 500 points to record, closes above 37,000 for the first time: Live updates

-CNBC

1. The Dow Jones industrial Average reached a new record high as the Federal Reserve indicated multiple rate cuts next year.

2. The S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite also surged to fresh 52-week highs.

3. The Fed's meeting statement acknowledged easing inflation and lowered its inflation forecast for 2024.

Elon Musk's Tesla recalls two million cars in US over Autopilot defect

-BBC.com

1. Tesla is recalling over two million cars in the US due to defective Autopilot system.

2. The US regulator found that Tesla's Autopilot feature was not effective in preventing driver misuse.

3. Tesla will send a software update over the air to fix the issue, but the recall applies to almost every Tesla sold in the US since 2015.

🤖 TECH NEWS

Apple to launch stolen iPhone protection feature in upcoming iOS security update

-WPVI-TV

1. Apple is implementing security changes to protect iPhone users against theft and unauthorized access to their accounts.

2. The new feature called "Stolen Device Protection" makes it more difficult for someone to change the Apple ID password when the user is not in a familiar location.

3. Users will need to enable the feature and it will be included in an upcoming iOS software update.

Meta's Ray-Ban Glasses Now Have Thoughts About Your Pants

-Gizmodo

1. Meta's Ray-Ban Smart Glasses now offer AI-enabled vision features, allowing the onboard AI to see, hear, and interpret the environment through the glasses' camera and microphone.

2. Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg showcased the AI capabilities by asking the glasses what pants to wear with a colorful shirt, receiving a somewhat unhelpful response.

3. The glasses also enable real-time search through Bing AI and have potential uses such as captioning photos or describing objects.

E3 Gaming Expo Is Shutting Down Permanently

-The New York Times

1. The annual video game convention E3 is permanently shutting down after struggling to stay relevant in a rapidly evolving industry.

2. E3, held in Los Angeles since 1995, was known for showcasing upcoming games to fans and industry professionals.

3. The convention faced competition from other fan-focused conventions and lost partners, leading to its decline.
