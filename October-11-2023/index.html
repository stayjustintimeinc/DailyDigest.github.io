Israel-Hamas War: Israel's New Unity Government Pledges to 'Crush' Hamas

-The New York Times

1. Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu forms an emergency government in Israel, adding two opposition lawmakers to his cabinet, with the aim of crushing Hamas.

2. Netanyahu and his cabinet present a united front and detail the atrocities committed by Hamas terrorists.

3. Israel intensifies its retaliation against Hamas, launching more missiles at Gaza, while fears of a wider conflict increase due to exchanges of fire along Israel's northern border.

U.S. intelligence indicates Iranian officials surprised by Hamas attack on Israel

-CBS News

1. U.S. intelligence indicates that senior Iranian officials were surprised by the Hamas-led terror attack on Israel.

2. Initial assessments suggest that Tehran was not directly involved in the launch of the deadly assault.

3. The U.S. is continuing to analyze the situation and collect more information regarding Iran's potential involvement.

China frees detained Australian journalist Cheng Lei

-NPR

1. Cheng Lei, an Australian journalist, has been allowed to leave China and return home after being detained for over three years on charges of espionage.

2. Cheng's arrest took place during a period of strained Australia-China relations, with Australia condemning China's handling of the COVID-19 pandemic and facing trade restrictions.

3. Cheng's release comes as Australia seeks to improve ties with China, with Prime Minister Anthony Albanese planning a visit and a delegation of federal ministers discussing trade and investment.

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

Caroline Ellison, star witness in Bankman-Fried trial, recounts living in 'a constant state of dread'

-NBC News

1. An ex-girlfriend of tech entrepreneur Sam Bankman-Fried is testifying as the star witness in his criminal trial.

2. Bankman-Fried is facing federal charges, including wire fraud, securities fraud, and money laundering.

3. The ex-girlfriend alleges that Bankman-Fried instructed her to steal $10 billion from FTX's customers to repay loans.

Biden proposes a ban on 'junk fees' — from concert tickets to hotel rooms

-NPR

1. The Biden administration is taking action to eliminate hidden and surprise fees, known as "junk fees," in various industries.

2. The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) is proposing a rule that would require companies to disclose all mandatory costs upfront and could penalize violators. The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) is also ordering banks and credit unions to provide basic information to customers without charging fees.

3. Consumer advocates support the crackdown on junk fees, estimating that these fees cost buyers over $64 billion annually. However, critics, such as the U.S. Chamber of Commerce, argue that the regulations may harm consumers and interfere with business transactions.

Don't Sweat A Hot CPI Inflation Report; Federal Reserve Rate-Hike Odds Dive

-Investor's Business Daily

1. The upsurge in the 10-year Treasury yield has reduced the likelihood of a rate hike on November 1, according to Federal Reserve policymakers.

2. The core PCE price index, the Fed's primary inflation rate, has shown a significant easing of price pressures over the past three months.

3. The consumer price index (CPI) may be less important to investors compared to the PCE, as the CPI's weight on housing makes it less representative of consumer outlays.
