FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day in history, November 18, in 1913, the United States patent for the "Bra" was granted to Mary Phelps Jacob. She had devised the first modern bra by using two handkerchiefs and a ribbon. Her innovative design revolutionized women's undergarments, providing a more comfortable alternative to the restrictive corsets of the era. Today, bras have become an essential item for women worldwide, catering to various shapes, sizes, and styles.

Milei echoes Trump with fraud claims that inject uncertainty into Argentina's presidential runoff

-POLITICO

1. Argentine presidential candidate Javier Milei is following the playbook of former US President Donald Trump, claiming election results are dubious and that he may be deprived of the nation's top job.

2. Milei's message resonates with Argentines discontented with rising poverty and inflation, and he has stoked claims of fraud to fire up his base and promote vigilance at polling stations.

3. While some supporters believe Milei's claims of fraud, fact-checking agencies have shown that the alleged irregularities in the voting process are comparable for both Milei and his opponent, Economy Minister Sergio Massa.

Biggest protest in Spain against Catalan amnesty law draws 170000

-Reuters

1. Approximately 170,000 people participated in a protest in Madrid against an amnesty law agreed upon by Spain's Socialists over Catalonia's separatist bid in 2017.

2. The protesters expressed concerns that the amnesty law threatens the rule of law and the separation of powers, with many holding signs and waving Spanish flags.

3. The amnesty is set to cover around 400 people involved in the independence bid, including separatists and police involved in clashes, and is considered the largest in Spain since the 1977 amnesty and the first in the European Union since 1991.

Ana Clara Benevides DIES of cardiac arrest while attending Taylor Swift concert in sweltering stadium

-Daily Mail

1. A Taylor Swift fan died during her concert in Brazil, believed to have had a cardiac arrest after fainting.

2. The concert took place in extreme heat, with temperatures reaching 100F, leading to a local heat warning.

3. Swift stopped her show to plead with stadium staff to provide water to concertgoers who were seen to be in desperate need.

The Fear and Tension That Led to Sam Altman's Ouster at OpenAI

-The New York Times

1. Sam Altman, the chief executive of OpenAI, was pushed out of his role due to tensions within the company regarding the potential dangers of OpenAI's technology and concerns about his leadership.

2. The ouster highlights a rift in the AI community between those who view AI as a significant business opportunity and those who fear the risks associated with its rapid development.

3. OpenAI's culture has always prioritized the consideration of AI risks, but the specific reasons for Altman's removal have not been disclosed by the company's board.

Elon Musk vows 'thermonuclear lawsuit' as advertisers flee X over antisemitism

-The Independent

1. Elon Musk vows to file a "thermonuclear lawsuit" against a US media watchdog that accused Twitter of placing advertisements alongside antisemitic posts.

2. Major brands like Disney, Apple, IBM, and Comcast's Xfinity have suspended their advertising on Twitter following the allegations made by the media watchdog.

3. Elon Musk claims that many of the largest advertisers are suppressing freedom of speech and threatens to take legal action against Media Matters and those involved in the alleged attack on his company.

SpaceX Starship launch failed minutes after reaching space

-Reuters

1. SpaceX's Starship spacecraft failed in space during its second test mission, further than the previous attempt which ended in an explosion.

2. The Super Heavy first stage booster of the rocket exploded over the Gulf of Mexico shortly after detaching, while the core Starship stage lost contact with mission control.

3. The Federal Aviation Administration will oversee an investigation into the testing failure and SpaceX will need to seek approval for future launches.

Nothing pulls its iMessage app from the Play Store following privacy disaster

-Android Police

1. Nothing Chats' security protocols were non-existent, raising concerns about privacy and data protection.

2. The platform was found to be sending credentials over plain text HTTP instead of HTTPS, compromising user privacy.

3. 9to5Google discovered that messages, media, and personal data were being logged and stored in plain text, making them publicly accessible.

Alan Wake II is great, but it doesn't need guns

-Engadget

1. Alan Wake II is a highly acclaimed game praised for its paranormal murder mystery and stunning visuals.

2. The game features two playable characters, Saga Anderson and Alan Wake, who must solve mysteries in a universe of broken realities.

3. While the game is highly enjoyable, some critics believe that the gunplay segments interrupt the slow-burning narrative tension and feel out of place in the survival horror experience.
