Why Americans feel gloomy about the economy despite falling inflation and low unemployment

-The Associated Press

1. Despite low inflation, steady employment rates, and an economy defying recession predictions, most Americans view the economy as poor according to polls and surveys.

2. Economists attribute the disconnect to the lingering effects of high inflation, with prices remaining high and rising, particularly for essential goods and services.

3. Americans want prices to return to pre-pandemic levels, and economists suggest the goal should be for wages to rise faster than prices to ensure consumers benefit.
