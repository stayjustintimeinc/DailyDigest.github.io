FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day in history, December 18th, 1944, an unusual event occurred during World War II known as the "Miracle on the Vistula." Despite the bitterly cold winter conditions, the Soviet Red Army managed to erect a makeshift bridge across the frozen Vistula River near Warsaw, Poland. This allowed for the successful crossing of nearly 180,000 troops, hundreds of tanks, and heavy artillery to continue their offensive against the German forces occupying the city. The miraculous bridge construction significantly aided the liberation of Warsaw, marking a turning point in the war.

Pope approves blessings for same-sex couples that must not resemble marriage

-Yahoo News

1. Pope Francis has formally approved the ability for Catholic priests to bless same-sex couples, aiming to promote inclusivity while maintaining the church's ban on gay marriage.

2. The Vatican's document emphasizes that these blessings should not be confused with the sacrament of marriage and should not be tied to specific Catholic celebrations or civil union ceremonies.

3. The document highlights the importance of allowing people seeking a transcendent relationship with God to receive His love and mercy, without being subject to an exhaustive moral analysis.

BP suspends Red Sea shipping amid attacks by Yemen's Houthi militants

-The Washington Post

1. BP has temporarily halted all shipments through the Red Sea due to security concerns amid attacks by anti-Israel militants.

2. The move by BP has caused oil prices to rise and has unsettled energy markets.

3. Other shipping companies, such as MSC, Maersk, CMA CGM, Hapag-Lloyd, and Evergreen Group, have also taken precautionary measures and are avoiding the Suez Canal as militants continue to target vessels.

Google's Stadia Controller salvage operation will run for another year

-Ars Technica

1. Google extends the period for converting Stadia Controllers to generic Bluetooth devices until December 31, 2024.

2. The Stadia Controller originally connected to the Internet via Wi-Fi, but with the service dead, the orphaned controllers needed to be converted to use Bluetooth for wireless connectivity.

3. The update process for the controllers happens entirely on a webpage using the "WebUSB" API, making it impossible for a third party to archive the updater for future use.
