How masked gunmen killed Hardeep Singh Nijjar

-The Washington Post

1. Hardeep Singh Nijjar, a Sikh temple president in Surrey, British Columbia, was killed in a brazen attack.

2. Nijjar's family and friends believe that the Indian government was behind the attack due to his involvement in the separatist Khalistan movement.

3. Canadian Prime Minister Justin Trudeau has stated that there are "credible allegations" linking Nijjar's killing to agents of the Indian government.

Russia's war in Ukraine: Live updates

-CNN

1. Russian Foreign Minister Sergei Lavrov discusses safety of Zaporizhzhia Nuclear Power Plant with International Atomic Energy Agency.

2. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky meets with Wall Street CEOs to discuss rebuilding the country's economy.

3. Russia intercepts 22 Ukrainian drone attacks, no casualties reported.

Nagorno-Karabakh: Azerbaijan and Armenia reach cease-fire deal

-The Associated Press

1. Azerbaijan claims full control of Nagorno-Karabakh region after local Armenian forces agree to disarm and disband under a Russia-mediated cease-fire.

2. Azerbaijan President Ilham Aliyev declares victory, stating that Azerbaijan has fulfilled all its tasks and restored sovereignty.

3. The fighting escalates the humanitarian crisis in Nagorno-Karabakh, as residents endure shortages and thousands seek refuge in Russian peacekeeper camps.

Fed Holds Rates Steady but Pencils In One More Hike This Year

-The Wall Street Journal

1. Federal Reserve officials voted to hold interest rates steady at a 22-year high.

2. There is a divide among officials over whether they should raise rates again this year.

Americans can again order free COVID tests as Biden administration revives program

-Anchorage Daily News

1. The Biden administration is providing $600 million in funding to produce new at-home COVID-19 tests.

2. Americans will be able to order up to four free tests per household through the website COVIDTests.gov starting on Sept 25.

3. Twelve manufacturers have been awarded funding to produce 200 million over-the-counter tests to replenish federal stockpiles and meet the demand for tests ordered online.

UAW strike latest: GM sends 2000 workers home in Kansas

-NPR

1. General Motors has laid off around 2,000 unionized workers at its Kansas assembly plant due to the ongoing UAW strikes.

2. The UAW's targeted plant strikes have resulted in smaller impacts on the auto industry than expected, as compared to company-wide disruptions from previous engine plant strikes.

3. The pressure on automakers could increase as the UAW threatens to expand strikes to additional facilities unless significant concessions are made by Friday.
