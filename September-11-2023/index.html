On this day, September 11th, in 1940, the 'Battle of Britain' entered its final phase during World War II. On this significant day, the German Luftwaffe launched intensive air raids targeting London, known as the "Blitz." These sustained bombings were part of the German strategy to weaken British morale and force surrender, but the British, supported by their courageous pilots, successfully defended their homeland. The Battle of Britain ultimately ended in victory for the Allied forces, marking a turning point in the war against Nazi Germany.

-CBS News
