🌎 WORLD NEWS

For Palestinians Trapped in Gaza, There Is No Way Out

-The Wall Street Journal

1. Palestinians trapped in Gaza are desperately trying to flee ahead of an anticipated Israeli ground invasion.

2. However, they are finding that there is nowhere to run, as the Gaza Strip is running short of basic necessities like electricity, food, fuel, and water.

3. The dire situation in Gaza highlights the ongoing humanitarian crisis faced by its residents.

The family of a 24-year-old killed by Hamas at the Supernova music festival asked for 10 strangers to attend her funeral. Thousands showed up.

-CBS News

1. Bruna Valeanu, a 24-year-old student from Brazil, was killed in an attack at the Supernova music festival in Israel.

2. Valeanu's family, who recently moved to Israel, didn't know if they could hold a prayer service for her funeral due to not knowing many people.

3. Tens of thousands of people showed up to pay their respects at Valeanu's funeral, exceeding the minimum requirement of 10 people for a Jewish religious service.

France bans all pro-Palestinian protests

-CNN

1. Demonstrators in Paris gathered in defiance of a ban on pro-Palestinian rallies, leading to clashes with police using tear gas and water cannons.

2. The ban on pro-Palestinian rallies was imposed by the French Interior Minister, citing concerns about public order amidst the ongoing Gaza crisis.

3. Protests in support of both Israelis and Palestinians have been seen worldwide, with security measures increased in European countries to protect Jewish communities.

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

Inflation Slowdown Remains Bumpy, September Consumer Price Data Shows

-The New York Times

1. Consumer prices grew at the same pace in September as in August, with the Consumer Price Index climbing 3.7 percent from a year earlier.

2. Despite the slower price increases compared to last year, signs of stalling progress in inflation suggest caution for Federal Reserve officials.

3. The recent inflation report showed less progress than expected, leading to potential concerns for investors and discussions among policymakers about another potential rate increase.

Inflation is already racing past next year’s Social Security COLA

-MarketWatch

1. Social Security beneficiaries will receive a 3.2% cost-of-living adjustment to their monthly checks starting in January 2024.

2. Consumer prices have risen by 0.4% between August and September, indicating an annual rate of 4.9%.

3. Prices for services other than rent are now rising at an alarming annual rate of 7.4%, three times the rate from a couple of months ago.

Bank earnings kick off with JPMorgan, Wells Fargo amid concerns about rising rates, bad loans

-CNBC

1. Higher interest rates are causing concerns for American banks, resulting in shrinking margins and rising loan losses.

2. The surge in interest rates is expected to lead to losses on banks' bond portfolios and funding pressures due to higher rates for deposits.

3. Analysts estimate that banks' per-share earnings fell 18% in the third quarter due to compressed lending margins and lower loan demand resulting from higher borrowing costs.

Galaxy Note 20 series is next in line for the October 2023 update - SamMobile

-SamMobile - Samsung news

1. Samsung is rolling out the October 2023 security patch for its Galaxy Note 20 series phones, including both LTE and 5G variants. The update is available in multiple countries across Europe and the USA.

2. The Galaxy Note 20 Ultra model is also receiving the October 2023 security patch through firmware updates. The update is available for both the international version and carrier-bound versions in the USA.

3. Galaxy Note 20 and Note 20 Ultra users can download the October 2023 update by going to the Settings app on their phones and selecting "Software update." They can also manually install the update using firmware files from the Samsung website and a PC.

Ubuntu 23.10 is a Minotaur that moves faster and takes up less space

-Ars Technica

1. Ubuntu 23.10 introduces changes to the installer, with a new "Default installation" option that focuses on essentials and basic utilities. The ISO file size is slightly smaller than the previous release.

2. The App Center, Ubuntu's software portal, has been upgraded and rewritten in Flutter for faster performance. The new interface prioritizes containerized Snap apps.

3. Other upgrades include a dedicated firmware updater tool, improvements to fractional scaling and tiling in GNOME 45, and the inclusion of new features in the 6.5 kernel, such as support for Xbox controller rumble and audio for Asus ROG Ally.
