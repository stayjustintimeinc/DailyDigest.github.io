FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day in history, September 3rd, 1882, the very first United States Labor Day parade took place in New York City. It was organized by the Central Labor Union to celebrate the achievements and contributions of American workers. The parade featured over 10,000 participants marching through the streets, unveiling the spirit of solidarity among workers and highlighting the importance of their rights. Today, Labor Day is widely celebrated in the United States on the first Monday of September, honoring the social and economic achievements of American workers.

NYC Airbnb crackdown squeezes hosts and renters starting September 5

-Business Insider

1. Starting from September 5, New York City will enforce a law requiring hosts on short-term rental platforms like Airbnb and Vrbo to register with the city, imposing new rules such as a ban on entire-unit rentals and a reservation cap of two people.

2. Non-compliance with the new rules could result in fines of up to $5,000 per stay, potentially leading to the closure of thousands of short-term rentals in the city.

3. New York City estimates there are up to 10,800 illegal listings across all short-term-rental platforms, and the city has approved only 257 licenses so far, out of an estimated 40,000 listings on Airbnb alone.
