FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day, November 29, in 1961, the Mercury-Atlas 5 mission successfully launched a chimpanzee named Enos into space. Enos became the first chimp to complete two orbits around the Earth, paving the way for human space missions. This extraordinary achievement in space exploration helped NASA gain valuable data and confidence for future human spaceflights.

Israel-Hamas war live: Hamas says 10-month-old hostage and two family members killed in Israeli bombardment

-The Guardian

1. The armed wing of Hamas claims that a 10-month-old baby, along with his older brother and mother, were killed during the Israeli bombardment of Gaza.

2. The Israeli army is checking the claim made by Hamas, while independent verification is not yet available.

3. The US is hopeful that the truce between Israel and Hamas can be extended, and efforts are being made to continue the deal.

US military Osprey aircraft with 6 aboard crashes off southern Japan, at least 1 dead

-The Associated Press

1. A U.S. Air Force Osprey aircraft crashed during a training mission off the southern coast of Japan.

2. One crew member was found dead, and the status of the remaining seven crew members is unknown.

3. The cause of the crash is not yet known, and the Japanese coast guard is conducting search and rescue operations.

COP28 host UAE lashes out at ‘fake news’ on eve of the major UN climate summit

-CNBC

1. The United Arab Emirates (UAE) has denounced "fake news" aimed at undermining its role as host of the COP28 climate conference.

2. Press releases falsely claiming that the president-designate of COP28 would step down from his position at ADNOC (Abu Dhabi National Oil Co.) were circulated on social media.

3. The official COP28 UAE account clarified that the press release was fake and warned that official news will only be published through verified social media and the press office.

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

US economic growth revised up to 5.2% in third quarter

-Fox Business

1. The U.S. economy grew at a faster pace of 5.2% in the third quarter, compared to the previously reported 4.9% increase.

2. The upward revision in government and business spending contributed to the stronger growth, while consumer spending was revised down to a 3.6% rate.

3. However, there are signs of a slowing economy, including moderating job growth, a downturn in the housing market, and cooling consumer spending.

Why Warren Buffett wouldn't have become the greatest investor ever without Charlie Munger

-CNBC

1. Warren Buffett credits Charlie Munger for guiding him towards investing in strong-brand quality companies instead of cheap failing ones.

2. Charlie Munger's influence helped Buffett build Berkshire Hathaway into an empire of first-class businesses.

3. Munger broke Buffett's habit of investing in discounted but low-quality companies, leading to the success of Berkshire Hathaway.

Why Bill Ackman thinks the economy will soon need a Fed rate cut

-MarketWatch

1. Hedge-fund manager Bill Ackman believes the Fed should start cutting rates as early as Q1 2024 due to a weakening economy.

2. Ackman warns of a potential hard landing if the Fed doesn't act soon, citing a high real rate of interest and the need for debt repricing.

3. Not everyone agrees with Ackman's view, with some arguing that current real yields are normal and the economy is not being negatively impacted.

Don't Be Afraid of the iPhone's NameDrop Feature, Experts Say

-The New York Times

1. Police departments have raised concerns about Apple's NameDrop feature that allows users to exchange contact information wirelessly. However, tech experts argue that these fears are overblown as NameDrop requires the devices to be practically touching and both users must agree to share the information.

2. NameDrop is a default setting in the latest Apple operating systems - iOS 17.1 for the iPhone and WatchOS 10.1 for the Apple Watch. Users can hold their devices close together until NameDrop appears on both screens and choose to exchange contact information.

3. Unlike previous technologies like AirDrop, it is difficult to send unwanted information or harvest personal details without consent using NameDrop. Only the contact details added to the user's contact cards are shared, and both users must have NameDrop enabled and agree to share information.
