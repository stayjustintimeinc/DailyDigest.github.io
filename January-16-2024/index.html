FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

Fun Fact: On this day, January 16th, in 1909, the first fully synthetic plastic, Bakelite, was patented by Belgian-born American chemist Leo Hendrik Baekeland. Bakelite revolutionized the manufacturing industry and became wildly popular due to its electrical nonconductivity and heat-resistant properties. This breakthrough marked the beginning of the Plastic Age, an era that greatly influenced modern life as we know it today.

US military seizes Iranian missile parts bound for Houthi rebels in raid where 2 SEALs went missing

-Yahoo News

US condemns Iran for 'reckless' missile strikes on northern Iraq

-BBC.com

3. The Revolutionary Guards claimed to target an Israeli "spy headquarters" in Iraq, while also carrying out missile strikes on "terrorist bases" in Syria in response to a suicide bombing claimed by ISIS.

Burger King owner Restaurant Brands buys chain's largest U.S. franchisee

-CNBC

1. Restaurant Brands International is acquiring Carrols Restaurant Group, the largest Burger King franchisee in the U.S., for approximately $1 billion in cash.

2. Carrols operates over 1,000 Burger King restaurants and 60 Popeyes locations.

3. This acquisition marks a shift in strategy for Burger King, as it aims to revive its U.S. business by investing in restaurant remodels and driving demand through advertising.

WA attorney general sues to block Kroger, Albertsons merger

-OregonLive

1. Washington state has filed a lawsuit to block the proposed merger between Kroger and Albertsons, citing concerns of harm to consumers and raised prices.

2. The suit argues that the merger between Kroger and Albertsons, which account for over half of all grocery sales in Washington, would limit competition and lead to higher prices and less variety.

3. A plan by Kroger and Albertsons to sell locations to a third company, C&S Wholesale Grocers, is deemed inadequate to restore lost competition, as C&S lacks experience operating pharmacies and fueling stations.
