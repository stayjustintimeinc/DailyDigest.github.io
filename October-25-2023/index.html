UN says fuel shortages will halt Gaza aid operations within a day

-CNN

1. The United Nations Relief and Works Agency for Palestine Refugees in the Near East (UNRWA) will have to halt aid operations within a day if fuel is not delivered to Gaza. This would be a significant blow to civilians who rely on the organization for essential services.

2. Israel has refused to allow fuel to enter Gaza since Hamas' attack, citing concerns that it would be used by the militant group for its fight against Israel. The lack of fuel is severely affecting the ability to deliver aid, power hospitals, and provide clean water.

3. The current supplies of aid are insufficient for the needs of the 2 million Palestinians in Gaza, and without fuel, the situation will worsen. Hospitals are on the brink of shutting down, and the lack of sanitation and clean water raises concerns of a health crisis.

Japan's Supreme Court hands down a landmark transgender rights decision

-NPR

1. Japan's Supreme Court rules that the requirement for sterilization surgery for people seeking to legally change their gender is unconstitutional.

2. The decision is seen as a significant victory for LGBTQ+ rights in Japan, but the plaintiff's fate remains uncertain as her case is sent back to a lower court.

3. The court acknowledges that the current rules force individuals to choose between surgery and abandoning their decision to change genders, and that requiring sterilization violates constitutional guarantees of bodily autonomy and freedom from invasion into the body against their will.

Turkey's Erdogan says Hamas is not terrorist organisation, cancels trip to Israel

-Reuters

1. Turkish President Tayyip Erdogan stated that Hamas is not a terrorist organization but a liberation group fighting to protect Palestinian lands. Turkey does not consider Hamas a terrorist organization and hosts members of the group on its territory.

2. Erdogan criticized Western powers for supporting Israel's bombing of Gaza and called for an immediate ceasefire, the entry of humanitarian aid into Gaza, and for Muslim countries to work together to stop the violence.

3. Erdogan's comments drew criticism from Israel and Italian Deputy Prime Minister Matteo Salvini. Shares in Istanbul dropped 7% following Erdogan's remarks on Hamas. Erdogan also announced the suspension of Turkey's efforts to mend ties with Israel.

U.A.W. and Ford Reach Tentative Contract Agreement

-The New York Times

1. The United Automobile Workers and Ford Motor have reached a tentative agreement on a new four-year labor contract, including a 25% pay increase over four years and major gains on pensions and job security.

2. The union called on striking Ford workers to go back to work while the tentative agreement awaits ratification by the U.A.W. council. If approved, the contract terms will be submitted to Ford's 57,000 union workers for their verdict.

3. The deal with Ford could increase pressure on General Motors and Stellantis to reach agreements with the union. The companies are investing in battery-powered vehicles and claim it makes it harder for them to afford substantially higher wages.

3. Alphabet's Class-A shares had their worst day since March 2020, dropping by 9.5%, after the company reported revenue below analyst estimates in its Google cloud unit.
