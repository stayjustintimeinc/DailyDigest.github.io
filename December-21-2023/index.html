🌎 WORLD NEWS

Prague shooting: At least 15 people killed by university student

-The Associated Press

1. A lone gunman opened fire in a university building in downtown Prague, killing at least 14 people and injuring over 20 others.

2. The shooter was a student at Charles University and also suspected in the killing of his father earlier in the day, as well as another man and his 2-month-old daughter a few days prior.

3. Authorities do not suspect a link to any extremist ideology or groups, and there is no indication that the massacre is connected to international terrorism.

US has serious concerns over UN vote on suspending fighting between Israel and Hamas, humanitarian aid to Gaza

-CNN

1. The US is expressing concerns over a draft resolution for a suspension in fighting and increased humanitarian assistance for Gaza, citing potential delays in aid delivery due to a proposed UN monitoring mechanism.

2. A US veto could prevent the resolution from passing, putting it in continued limbo.

3. US officials are discussing the resolution and the potential veto, while also urging Israel to take steps to protect innocent lives during the conflict.

Container rates hit $10,000 as ocean freight inflation soars in Red Sea crisis

-CNBC

1. The global supply chain is facing challenges due to rising ocean and air freight prices and stranded cargo.

2. Ocean freight prices have sharply increased as more vessels divert from the Red Sea, reaching $10,000 per 40-foot container from Shanghai to the U.K.

3. Truck rates in the Middle East have more than doubled, contributing to the overall increase in logistics costs.

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

Debt-laden Warner Bros. Discovery and Paramount consider merger

-Ars Technica

1. The CEOs of Warner Bros. Discovery (WBD) and Paramount Global have held discussions about a potential merger.

2. Talks are still in the early stages, and no formal negotiations have taken place yet.

3. A merger between the two companies could help bolster WBD's streaming portfolio and create a more competitive player in the media industry.

Was stock market's midweek dip a 'one-day wonder' or start of 'decent pullback'?

-MarketWatch

1. Wall Street experienced a sudden downdraft, leading to a significant drop in stock prices. Technical analyst Mark Arbeter suggests that this could either be a one-day event or the start of a significant pullback.

2. Major indexes had been overbought, but some indicators, such as breadth and momentum divergence, were still relatively strong. This indicates that the bull market may have further to go, despite the potential for a near-term pullback.

3. The article mentions previous instances of market breadth thrusts, which typically occur in the early or middle stages of a bull market. While there is a possibility of a pullback, the overall outlook suggests that the bull market still has room to grow.

Boeing hands over first 787 Dreamliner to China since 2019

-CNBC

1. Boeing delivered a 787 Dreamliner directly to a Chinese airline for the first time since November 2019.

2. The delivery of the 787 Dreamliner could potentially open up the possibility of deliveries of the Boeing 737 Max, which has been grounded in China since March 2019.

3. Over one-third of Boeing's inventory of approximately 250 Max planes are dedicated to Chinese airlines.

🤖 TECH NEWS

Beeper says it's done playing cat and mouse with Apple over its iMessage for Android app

-Engadget

1. Beeper has released another fix after Apple disabled access to iMessage, but claims this will be the last fix.

2. The latest workaround involves pairing your current mobile device with a Mac or an old iPhone to access Beeper.

3. If the final fix is blocked by Apple, Beeper Mini will no longer be available, but the software is open-source for others to use.

Lapsus$ hacker behind GTA 6 leak gets indefinite hospital sentence

-BleepingComputer

1. Arion Kurtaj, a member of the Lapsus$ cybercrime group, has been sentenced indefinitely to a secure hospital by a UK judge for his involvement in leaking assets related to the upcoming video game Grand Theft Auto VI.

2. Kurtaj, who is 18 years old and autistic, is considered a high risk to the public due to his abilities and desire to commit cybercrime. He will remain in the secure hospital until cleared by doctors.

3. Another unnamed 17-year-old Lapsus$ member involved in breaching tech giants NVIDIA and telcos and attempting to extort them has been sentenced to a Youth Rehabilitation Order with strict supervision, including a ban on using VPNs online.

Apple wants AI to run directly on its hardware instead of in the cloud

-Ars Technica

1. Apple's latest research focuses on running large language models on smartphones, indicating its intention to catch up with competitors in generative artificial intelligence.

2. The research paper titled "LLM in a Flash" offers a solution to the current computational bottleneck, enabling effective inference of LLMs on devices with limited memory.

3. By developing AI that can run directly on iPhones, Apple aims to provide a new user experience, anticipate user actions, and introduce new photo editing techniques while maintaining privacy benefits.
