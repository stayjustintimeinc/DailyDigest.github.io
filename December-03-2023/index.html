FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day, December 3rd, in 1967, the first successful heart transplant operation was performed by South African surgeon Dr. Christiaan Barnard. The groundbreaking procedure took place at Groote Schuur Hospital in Cape Town, where Dr. Barnard and his team replaced the damaged heart of a 53-year-old grocer with the heart of a young accident victim. The recipient survived the surgery for 18 days, pioneering a medical milestone that revolutionized the field of cardiac transplantation.

-The Guardian

1. The Pentagon is investigating reports of a drone attack on a US warship and commercial vessels in the Red Sea, believed to be carried out by Houthi rebels in Yemen.

Venezuelans vote to decide future of disputed territory with Guyana

-The Associated Press

1. Venezuelans are voting in a referendum to claim sovereignty over a disputed territory, Essequibo, controlled by Guyana.

3. Guyana sees the referendum as a step towards annexation and is concerned about the outcome of the vote.
