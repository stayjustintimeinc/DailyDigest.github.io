FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day, January 17th, in 1929, the famous cartoon character Popeye the Sailor Man made his debut in the comic strip "Thimble Theatre" by Elzie Segar. Created as a side character, Popeye quickly gained enormous popularity and eventually became the main focus of the strip. His love for spinach and his iconic catchphrases, such as "I yam what I yam" and "That's all I can stands, and I can't stands no more," have made Popeye one of the most enduring and beloved characters in cartoon history.

Iran says attack in Pakistan targeted ‘Iranian terrorist group’

-Al Jazeera English

1. Two children were killed and three others injured in Iranian air attacks in Pakistan targeting the Jaish al-Adl group.

2. Iran's Foreign Minister, Hossein Amir-Abdollahian, claimed that the attacks were in response to the group's recent attacks on the Iranian city of Rask.

3. Pakistan recalled its ambassador to Iran and declared the airspace violation as unacceptable, reserving the right to respond to the illegal act.

Britain's King Charles III to undergo hospital treatment for an enlarged prostate

-NBC News

1. King Charles III of Britain will undergo a corrective procedure for an enlarged prostate.

2. His condition is benign and he will attend the hospital next week for the procedure.

3. The king's public engagements will be postponed for a short period of recuperation.

Statement from National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan on the Terrorist Designation of the Houthis

-The White House

1. The United States has designated Ansarallah (also known as the Houthis) as a Specially Designated Global Terrorist in response to their attacks on US military forces and international maritime vessels in the Red Sea and Gulf of Aden. This designation aims to impede terrorist funding to the Houthis, restrict their financial access, and hold them accountable for their actions.

2. The US will reevaluate the designation if the Houthis cease their attacks in the Red Sea and Gulf of Aden. The designation will take effect 30 days from now with robust humanitarian carve outs in place to ensure it targets the Houthis and not the people of Yemen.

3. The US is implementing carve outs and licenses to prevent adverse impacts on the Yemeni people. Commercial shipments into Yemeni ports, which the Yemeni people rely on for food, medicine, and fuel, will continue and are not covered by the sanctions. Furthermore, the US includes carve outs for food, medicine, and humanitarian assistance in all sanctions programs. President Biden has emphasized that the US will take further actions to protect its people and the flow of international commerce.

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

Tesla Drivers in Chicago Confront a Harsh Foe: Cold Weather

-The New York Times

1. Electric vehicle owners in Chicago are experiencing difficulties due to freezing temperatures, as batteries become less efficient and have shorter range.

2. Cold weather increases energy consumption in electric vehicles, as more energy is required to heat batteries and the cabin.

3. Tesla has provided tips to help drivers mitigate the impact of freezing temperatures, such as keeping the charge level above 20% and using the "scheduled departure" feature to optimize charging and preconditioning.

US FAA says first 40 inspections of Boeing 737 MAX 9 airplanes complete

-Yahoo Finance

1. The Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) has completed inspections on an initial group of 40 Boeing 737 MAX 9 planes.

2. The results of the inspections will be reviewed by the FAA to determine if it is safe to allow the MAX 9s to resume flying.

3. Alaska Airlines and United Airlines, the two U.S. airlines using the MAX 9, have had to cancel flights due to the grounding of the planes.

Retail sales rise faster than expected in December during key holiday season

-Fox Business

1. Retail sales in December rose by 0.6%, surpassing economists' expectations of a 0.4% gain. Excluding volatile measurements, sales still climbed 0.6%.

2. Consumers continued to spend on car dealers, grocery stores, online retailers, building and garden supply stores, and clothing stores. However, spending declined at gas stations, health and personal care stores, electronics and appliance stores, and furniture stores.

3. Despite high inflation, consumer spending remained strong due to a solid job market and wage increases. However, concerns arise regarding the increasing reliance on credit cards and the potential for a holiday debt hangover.
