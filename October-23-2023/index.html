Israel War Live News: Hamas Releases 2 More Hostages as Gaza Death Toll Rises

-The New York Times

1. Two women, Nurit Cooper and Yocheved Lifshitz, were released by Hamas after being abducted in a massacre in Kibbutz Nir Oz. Their husbands remain in captivity, along with over 200 other hostages.

2. The release of the two women was attributed to humanitarian and health reasons, following the recent release of an Israeli-American mother and daughter. The hope is that this marks the beginning of further releases.

3. The death toll in Gaza has risen sharply, and Israel has attacked Hezbollah positions in Lebanon amidst international efforts to prevent the conflict from spreading. Aid workers have started distributing relief supplies, but the conditions in Hamas-run territory remain dire.

Intelligence shows Iranian-backed militias are ready to ramp up their attacks against US forces in the Middle East

-CNN

1. Iranian-backed militia groups are planning to increase attacks against US forces in the Middle East, taking advantage of the regional backlash to US support for Israel.

2. The US has specific intelligence that these militia groups could escalate their attacks as the conflict between Israel and Hamas continues.

3. Iran is providing guidance to the militia groups, encouraging them to attack US and Israeli targets with the promise of no punishment.

Turkey's Erdogan submits Sweden's NATO bid to parliament for ratification, presidency says

-Reuters

1. Turkish President Erdogan submits a bill approving Sweden's NATO membership bid to parliament for ratification, clearing the way for Sweden to join the Western defense alliance. Erdogan had previously raised objections but now believes that Sweden has taken sufficient steps to address Turkey's concerns.

2. Sweden's Prime Minister welcomes the move, and NATO Secretary General looks forward to a "speedy vote" in the Turkish parliament and welcoming Sweden as an ally soon. Hungary's parliament will also need to ratify Sweden's NATO application.

3. The United States also welcomes Turkey's move and looks forward to the bill passing in the Turkish parliament. Turkey has not set a timeframe for ratification, and the bill will be put on the agenda of parliament's foreign affairs commission before it can be sent to the general assembly for ratification.

Jim Cramer says there is a bull market in oil and gas thanks to recent big mergers

-CNBC

1. CNBC's Jim Cramer sees a bull market in oil and gas, with increasing value in oil stocks due to recent mergers and rising crude prices.

2. Chevron's acquisition of Hess for $53 billion and Exxon Mobil's purchase of Pioneer Natural Resources for $59.5 billion are reminiscent of oil takeovers in the 1980s.

3. Cramer believes Chevron can make deals that bring immediate earnings growth, citing Coterra and Conoco as examples, and suggests staying invested in oil stocks despite concerns about ESG and electric vehicle sales.

UAW strike expands again as Ram truck plant workers walk off the job

-NBC News

1. The United Auto Workers union has expanded its strike to include a plant that produces Ram 1500 trucks, targeting one of Stellantis' most important and profitable vehicles.

2. UAW President Shawn Fain criticized Stellantis for proposing weaker contract proposals compared to Ford and General Motors, including cuts to medical coverage and 401(k) contributions, and seeking the right to demand new concessions from workers.

3. Stellantis stated that negotiations with the union have been productive, offering improved terms such as 23% wage increases and increased retirement savings contributions, but the union has not provided a counter-offer since the latest talks.

Exclusive: Nvidia to make Arm-based PC chips in major new challenge to Intel

-Reuters

1. Nvidia is designing central processing units (CPUs) to run Windows operating system using Arm technology, in an effort to challenge Intel's stronghold on personal computers.

2. Microsoft is supporting chip companies in building Arm-based processors for Windows PCs, aiming to compete with Apple's Arm-based chips for Mac computers.

3. Nvidia, AMD, and Qualcomm all plan to sell PC chips with Arm technology, potentially shaking up the PC industry and putting pressure on Intel. Microsoft has encouraged chipmakers to integrate advanced AI features into the CPUs they are designing.
