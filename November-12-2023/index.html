FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day, November 12th, in 1990, the "Leaning Tower of Pisa" in Italy was closed to the public for the first time in over 800 years. A committee had deemed the iconic tower to be at risk of collapse due to its significant tilt. Extensive restoration efforts were undertaken to stabilize and straighten the tower, successfully reopening it to the public in December 2001.

🌎 WORLD NEWS

French politicians join 100,000 at Paris march against antisemitism

-The Guardian

1. Over 100,000 people, including politicians, marched in Paris against antisemitism amid a surge in anti-Jewish incidents in France.

2. The demonstration was peaceful, although minor scuffles occurred as a leftwing Jewish group tried to prevent far-right party leaders from joining.

3. The event divided France's political class, with the attendance of the far-right National Rally drawing widespread criticism, while leftist parties and trade unions distanced themselves from the far-right representatives.

Israel says Palestinian Authority in current form should not run Gaza

-Reuters

1. Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu expressed doubts about the Palestinian Authority (PA) taking charge of the Gaza Strip.

2. Netanyahu cited grievances with the PA's school curriculum and policies as reasons why they should not govern Gaza.

3. The future of Gaza remains uncertain, with concerns about divisions between the West Bank and Gaza, and the authority and support of Palestinian President Mahmoud Abbas.

Diwali in US: Americans embrace Indian culture with desi students

-Hindustan Times

1. The scale of Diwali celebrations in America has increased over the years, with the festival being celebrated in popular spots like Disneyland and Times Square.

2. Diwali will now also be a school holiday in New York, following a legislation announced by mayor Eric Adams.

3. Indian students in the US form communities to celebrate Diwali and their efforts to stay connected to their culture have brought together Americans and people from other countries to embrace other cultures.

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

Inflation, Walmart, Target highlight a consumer-focused week: What to watch

-Yahoo Finance

1. The stock market rebounded last week, with major US indexes reaching their highest closing levels in nearly two months.

2. The upcoming week will bring updates on the health of the US consumer, including the release of the October Consumer Price Index (CPI) report and corporate earnings from big box retailers and other consumer-focused companies.

3. Moody's changing its outlook on the US government's debt to "negative" from "stable" will draw investor attention, as elevated interest rates raise concerns about servicing the growing debt pile.

Elon Musk, Tesla Price Cuts Are a Losing Business Strategy

-Business Insider

1. Tesla has been engaged in a price war to compete with new electric-vehicle competitors, slashing prices by around 25%. However, lower prices have not translated into higher sales for Tesla, and the company's once high profit margins are being squeezed.

2. Tesla's market share in the US has decreased from 62% to 50%, and the public's demand for electric vehicles is not growing as fast as expected. This means Tesla is fighting for a shrinking market share.

3. Offering deep discounts on vehicles is seen as a desperate move by Elon Musk, and it comes at a bad time when legacy automakers can rely on sales of traditional combustion-engine vehicles as a safety net. Tesla's profitability is at risk, and cost-cutting measures may not be enough to secure margins.

One Road to Affordable EVs Passes Through This Man's Turf

-The Wall Street Journal

1. Jack Cheng believes that any company can make its own car or truck through outsourcing, similar to how companies like Sony and Apple outsource production to contract-manufacturing giants.

2. This outsourcing model has already been successful in consumer electronics, allowing companies to focus on design and marketing rather than manufacturing.

3. Cheng's vision could lead to more affordable electric vehicles by enabling smaller companies to enter the market without the need for large-scale production infrastructure.

🤖 TECH NEWS

4 futuristic things the Humane Ai Pin can do as a smartphone alternative

-Mashable

1. The Humane Ai Pin, a screenless AI wearable, has been unveiled, aiming to replace smartphones and interact with the world around the user.

2. The Ai Pin features a "laser ink display" that can project visuals onto the user's palm, as well as a voice command function called Ai Mic for searches, task-handling, and messaging.

3. Other notable features include real-time translation capabilities, privacy measures such as a trust light and personic speaker, and a price starting at $699 with orders starting in November 2022.

Your phone is the key to your digital life. Make sure you know what to do if you lose it.

-Vox.com

1. Losing your phone can have devastating consequences as it serves as a gateway to various digital accounts and information.

2. Many people do not adequately prepare for the possibility of losing their phone, despite the high number of incidents.

3. It is crucial to regularly back up your phone to prevent data loss, either by connecting it to a computer or using cloud storage options.

4 reasons smart glasses are the best accessory for handheld gaming

-XDA Developers

1. Smart glasses are becoming more accessible to consumers in 2023 and offer a bright and colorful virtual screen for portable gaming.

2. Smart glasses provide a superior gaming experience compared to handheld gaming consoles due to their larger and more immersive virtual screens.

3. Using smart glasses like the XREAL Air 2 or Viture One is a convenient and plug-and-play solution for connecting to devices like the Steam Deck, eliminating the need for a separate display and offering a comfortable gaming experience.
