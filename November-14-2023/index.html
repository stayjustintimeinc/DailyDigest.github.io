FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On November 14th, but in the year 1889, Nellie Bly set out to complete her record-breaking journey around the world. Inspired by Jules Verne's novel "Around the World in Eighty Days," Bly, an American journalist, aimed to beat the fictional character Phileas Fogg and circumnavigate the globe in even less time. She embarked on her journey from New York City and finished it on January 25th, 1890, accomplishing the astounding feat in just 72 days, 6 hours, 11 minutes, and 14 seconds. Nellie Bly's groundbreaking adventure drew significant attention to women's capabilities and became a significant moment in travel history.
