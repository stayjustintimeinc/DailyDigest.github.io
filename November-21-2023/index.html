This invention introduced a new era of entertainment, enabling people to listen to recorded sound for the first time.

🌎 WORLD NEWS

Hostage deal announcement between Israel and Hamas could come as soon as Tuesday

-CNN

1. Israel, Hamas, and the US are close to reaching a deal that would lead to the release of 50 women and children hostages taken by Hamas during a terror attack in exchange for a pause in fighting and the release of Palestinian prisoners.

2. The deal, which could be announced soon, would be a significant de-escalatory step for Israel and the first sustained pause in fighting since the conflict began.

3. The negotiations have involved discussions on aerial surveillance, the gathering of additional hostages, and the provision of aid to Gaza, including fuel for Hamas' operations.

North Korea claims it launched first spy satellite, promises more

-Reuters

1. North Korea successfully placed its first spy satellite in orbit, defying international condemnation.

2. South Korea and Japan could not immediately verify the launch and placement of the satellite.

3. North Korea plans to launch more spy satellites in the near future to enhance its military preparedness.

Only 2-state solution can bring real peace to Israel and Palestinians: China

-South China Morning Post

1. Chinese President Xi Jinping expresses support for the Palestinian people and calls for a ceasefire in Gaza.

2. China sends humanitarian assistance to Gaza, including funds and supplies, and calls for secure humanitarian corridors.

3. China's positions on Gaza align with its strategy to deepen alliances with non-Western led groups and promote diplomatic negotiations as a solution.

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

Binance Founder Changpeng Zhao Agrees to Step Down, Plead Guilty

-The Wall Street Journal

1. Binance CEO Changpeng Zhao plans to step down and plead guilty to violating U.S. anti-money-laundering requirements.

2. Binance, owned by Zhao, will also plead guilty and pay fines totaling $4.3 billion.

3. The deal may allow Binance to continue operating despite the legal charges.

NFL committed to X partnership as Elon Musk's social platform gets heat for hate speech

-CNBC

1. The National Football League (NFL) is continuing its partnership with X (formerly known as Twitter) despite the platform's ongoing issues with hate speech and antisemitism.

2. The NFL's media and business chief acknowledged the challenges faced by X in content moderation but stated that they are working with the platform because their fans are active users.

3. Companies like Apple and Disney have suspended advertising on X due to concerns about harmful content, and Elon Musk's controversial statements have further fueled the backlash against the platform.

Best Buy stock drops as it posts broad sales decline for Q3

-Yahoo Finance

1. Best Buy's Q3 results were impacted by consumer pullback on discretionary goods, leading to lower net sales than expected.

2. Sales of appliances, consumer electronics, computing, and mobile phones all dropped, but there was strength in entertainment products.

3. Best Buy plans to close around 15 to 20 stores per year and is exploring new offerings and store formats to adapt to changing consumer demands.

Sunbird is shutting down its iMessage app for Android

-The Verge

1. Sunbird, the iMessage app for Android, temporarily shuts down due to security concerns regarding its end-to-end encryption.

2. Sunbird, which launched in 2022, touted privacy features like no message data collection and no ads.

3. Reports suggest that Sunbird may not actually provide end-to-end encryption and that it stores media files on an external cloud service.

3. Samsung has been releasing stable Android 14 and One UI 6 updates on Mondays, and more devices are expected to receive the update soon.
