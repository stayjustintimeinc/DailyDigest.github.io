FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day, November 11th, in 1918, World War I came to an end with the signing of the Armistice agreement. The cessation of hostilities took effect at the 11th hour of the 11th day of the 11th month. This significant event marked the end of one of the deadliest conflicts in history, which involved more than 70 million military personnel and resulted in over 16 million deaths. Today, November 11th is observed as Veterans Day in the United States, paying tribute to all veterans who have served their country.

Gaza's Al-Shifa Hospital Struggles as Fighting Continues: Israel-Hamas War News

-The New York Times

1. The main hospital in Gaza City, Al-Shifa, is facing a power outage due to a lack of fuel and electricity, causing the deaths of patients, including a premature baby in an incubator.

2. Israeli forces have surrounded the hospital, leading to a dire situation for the patients and limited medical services. The hospital has relied on backup generators and a dwindling supply of fuel.

3. Israel claims that the hospitals are shielding Hamas military operations, resulting in the evacuation of four hospitals. However, attempts to leave Al-Shifa have been met with sniper fire, resulting in casualties.

London police arrest over 120 as pro-Palestinian rally draws counter-protests

-Reuters

1. Over 300,000 pro-Palestinian demonstrators marched in central London, leading to clashes with far-right counter-protesters.

2. Police arrested over 120 people, mainly right wing protesters, during the demonstrations.

3. Prime Minister Rishi Sunak condemned the violence, including "Hamas sympathizers" who participated in the rally.

More than 800 Sudanese reportedly killed in attack on Darfur town, UN says

-ABC News

1. Paramilitary troops and allied Arab militias attacked the town of Ardamata in Sudan's Darfur region, resulting in the death of over 800 people.

2. The attack is part of an ongoing conflict between the Sudanese military and the paramilitary Rapid Support Forces (RSF) since mid-April.

3. The RSF and their militias targeted the African Masalit tribe, going from house to house, killing and detaining people. Thousands of people have been displaced, with many fleeing to neighboring Chad.

Qualcomm ends partnership for connecting Android phones to Iridium satellites

-SpaceNews

1. Qualcomm is no longer planning to provide the technology for Android smartphones to directly connect to Iridium's satellites, as no smartphone makers have included Qualcomm's enabling technology in their devices.

2. The lack of adoption of Qualcomm's technology by smartphone makers may be due to concerns over the price and monetization models.

3. While Qualcomm is terminating its efforts with Iridium on a proprietary solution, the company remains open to working with the satellite operator on a standards-based approach.

Amazon is rumored to be planning to ditch Android on its Fire devices

-TechRadar

1. Amazon engineers are reportedly working on an alternative operating system named Vega that won't have Android in it for their devices.

2. The new software is described as "fairly advanced" and has already been tested on Fire TV streaming sticks.

3. Vega is expected to run on all of Amazon's hardware products and may also be available for in-car entertainment systems in the future.
