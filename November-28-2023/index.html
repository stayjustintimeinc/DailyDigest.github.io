FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day, November 28th, in 1966, the first geosynchronous communication satellite, named ATS-1 (Applications Technology Satellite 1), was launched into space by NASA. This groundbreaking satellite had the ability to remain fixed in orbit above a specific location, allowing for continuous communication between ground stations and the satellite. The deployment of ATS-1 marked a significant milestone in telecommunications and paved the way for future advancements in global communication systems.

All 41 workers trapped in a tunnel in India were rescued

-The Associated Press

1. All 41 construction workers trapped in a collapsed mountain tunnel in northern India have been successfully rescued.

2. The workers were trapped for over two weeks before rescuers were able to reach them.

3. The rescue operation involved drilling through debris to create a passageway for the workers to be pulled out.

Pope cancels trip to U.N. climate conference on doctors' orders after getting the flu

-NPR

1. Pope Francis cancels his trip to the U.N. climate conference in Dubai on doctor's orders due to recovering from flu and lung inflammation.

2. Francis had previously revealed his lung inflammation but had planned to go to Dubai, becoming the first pontiff to address a U.N. climate conference.

3. This is the second time the pope's frail health has led to the cancellation of a foreign trip, with a previous trip to Congo and South Sudan in 2022 being postponed due to knee inflammation.

Israel-Hamas War: Fifth Round of Hostages Released as Extended Truce Appears to Hold

-The New York Times

1. Hamas released 10 Israeli and dual national hostages and two Thai citizens on Tuesday, as part of the temporary cease-fire agreement.

2. The total number of captives released so far is 85, including three members of a family and Israeli women ranging from 36 to 84 years old.

3. The cease-fire has held since Friday, with daily releases of hostages from Gaza and prisoners from Israeli custody, despite some accusations of cease-fire violations.

Charles T. Munger, Much More Than Warren Buffett's No. 2, Dies at 99

-The New York Times

1. Charles T. Munger, billionaire partner of Warren E. Buffett, died at the age of 99. Munger had a significant influence at Berkshire Hathaway, with his investing approach being credited as the blueprint for the company's success. He advised Buffett to buy solid brand-name companies instead of troubled companies at deep discounts.

2. Munger and Buffett formed a partnership spanning over 50 years and built one of the most successful conglomerates in history. Berkshire Hathaway owns various well-known companies and holds stakes in corporate giants like Coca-Cola, American Express, IBM, and Wells Fargo.

3. Munger was known for his witty common-sense maxims, called Mungerisms, which were collected in books. He and Buffett had a close relationship, talking to each other for hours every day and hosting the famous Berkshire annual meeting in Omaha. Munger was seen as the moral compass of Berkshire Hathaway, advising on personnel issues in addition to investments.

Groundbreaking transatlantic flight using greener fuel takes off

-BBC.com

1. Virgin Atlantic successfully completes the first transatlantic flight using only sustainable aviation fuels (SAF) from London to New York.

2. SAF, derived from waste fats and corn production, can reduce lifecycle emissions up to 70% compared to traditional jet fuel.

3. Despite the milestone, the availability and cost of SAF remain challenges, and further advancements in alternative technologies are needed to achieve net-zero emissions in aviation.

M2 MacBook Air Gets Speed Boost With This Novel Cooling System

-MacRumors

1. Frore Systems installed its solid-state active cooling system, AirJet Mini, into a 15-inch MacBook Air without a fan and saw significant performance improvements over time as the laptop's throttling kicked in to regulate temperature.

2. The AirJet Mini requires modifications to be installed into the MacBook Air, including milling off 0.3mm from the laptop's lid and removing certain internal components.

3. The impact of the AirJet Mini on battery life and power consumption is still unclear, but the system is already in mass production and Frore Systems is developing larger and smaller versions for different devices.

Shenandoah County Sheriff warns parents about new iPhone feature

-WHSV

1. Authorities warn parents about the iPhone feature 'Name Drop' that automatically shares contact information between iPhones placed next to each other.

2. Shenandoah County Sheriff raises concerns about children and teenagers unknowingly sharing their personal information with strangers.

3. Apple assures users that they can disable 'Name Drop' and choose not to share their contact information, but worries persist about parents' awareness and children's understanding of the feature.

Google Drive users say Google lost their files; Google is investigating

-Ars Technica

1. Google is investigating "sync issues" for Google Drive for desktop after reports of data loss.

2. Users have reported that months of Drive data suddenly disappeared and files went back to a state from May 2023.

3. The issue is complicated by the different modes of operation in Drive for Desktop, with the File Stream mode potentially leading to permanent data loss.
