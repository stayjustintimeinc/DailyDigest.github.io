🌎 WORLD NEWS

Israel-Hamas war: UN warns of Gaza "graveyard for children" – DW – 11/06/2023

-DW (English)

1. UN Chief Antonio Guterres calls for an immediate humanitarian ceasefire in Gaza, describing it as a "graveyard for children."

2. US President Joe Biden discusses pauses in Israel's military offensive in Gaza to allow for humanitarian aid and potential hostage negotiations.

3. The Hamas-run health ministry in Gaza claims a death toll of over 10,000 people, and concerns are raised over the limited aid and fuel supplies in the region.

Moscow bombs Odesa after Ukraine destroys Russian warship in Crimea

-POLITICO Europe

1. Russian missile carrier destroyed in Ukrainian attack in Kerch shipyard.

2. Russia bombs Odesa region damaging art museum and warehouses.

3. Ukrainian forces target Russian ammo depot in occupied Donetsk region.

Prominent 22-year-old Palestinian protester Ahed Tamimi arrested by Israel on suspicion of "inciting violence"

-CBS News

1. Ahed Tamimi, a 22-year-old Palestinian activist, was arrested by the Israel Defense Forces for suspected incitement of violence and terrorist activities.

2. The IDF presented a screenshot from Tamimi's social media account as evidence of her incitement, though the authenticity of the account is yet to be independently verified.

3. Tamimi gained fame at 14 for biting an Israeli soldier and was later arrested for assaulting soldiers; her father expressed hope that her generation would continue the resistance against Israeli occupation.

🏦 BUSINESS NEWS

Bumble shares drop as founder Wolfe Herd to relinquish CEO role

-Reuters

1. Bumble founder Whitney Wolfe Herd will step down as CEO and hand over the role to Lidiane Jones, the CEO of Salesforce-owned Slack.

2. Bumble's shares dropped 10% following the announcement of the leadership change.

3. Wolfe Herd will remain as executive chair of Bumble, while Jones will take over as CEO on January 2nd.

Disney Names Hugh Johnston CFO

-Hollywood Reporter

1. The Walt Disney Co. has named Hugh Johnston, currently the vice chairman and CFO of PepsiCo, as its senior executive vp and CFO, effective December 4.

2. Johnston joins Disney at a crucial time as the company undergoes a transformative period under CEO Bob Iger, including the acquisition of the remaining stake in Hulu and a possible proxy battle with activist investor Nelson Peltz.

3. Johnston's contract includes a base salary of $2 million, a $4 million annual target bonus, and various stock awards and signing bonuses. His appointment also raises speculation about his potential as a successor to Iger.

U.S. military members' personal data being sold online for as little as 1 cent

-Axios

1. Personal data of thousands of active-duty and veteran U.S. military members can be purchased for as little as one cent per name through data broker websites.

2. The availability of this data puts military personnel and their families at risk of being targeted with various schemes, including blackmail and misinformation campaigns.

3. The researchers suggest that Congress should pass a comprehensive privacy law and regulatory agencies should develop rules to govern the purchase of military personnel data to address this issue.

Samsung expects the Galaxy S24 to sell like hotcakes thanks to AI

-Android Police

1. Samsung plans to integrate OpenAI's ChatGPT or Google Bard on its upcoming flagship phones to boost demand and aims to ship 35 million Galaxy S24 units in 2024.

2. The company has set the production volume for its Galaxy Z Fold 6 and Flip 6 series at 8.2 million units and aims for foldables to comprise one unit in every three flagships it sells.

3. Samsung hopes on-device AI integration with Microsoft or Google will help it retake the lead from Apple in the premium smartphone segment and deliver a superior user experience.

Google has issued a fix for Pixel phones with Android 14 multi-user bug

-The Verge

1. Google's November update for Pixel phones addresses a critical bug that affected devices with multiple user profiles, causing issues such as out of space errors and reboot loops.

2. The update will be rolled out starting today and continue over the next week to fix the bug. It should restore access to media for affected users, but data recovery may not be possible for devices stuck in a reboot cycle.

3. The bug specifically affected Pixel phones with multiple user profiles, including child users, guests, and restricted profiles. It did not impact devices with multiple Google accounts as the primary user. It is recommended to back up data before creating or logging into another user profile.
