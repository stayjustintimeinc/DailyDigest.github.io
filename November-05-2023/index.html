FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day, November 5th, in 1872, suffragist, abolitionist, and women's rights activist Susan B. Anthony cast her vote in the U.S. presidential election, becoming the first woman to do so. She had been arrested and put on trial earlier that year for illegally voting, as women's suffrage was not officially recognized at the time. Despite facing immense backlash, Anthony's fearless act laid the foundation for future advancements in women's rights and paved the way for the eventual ratification of the 19th Amendment in 1920, securing women's right to vote in the United States.

-Reuters
