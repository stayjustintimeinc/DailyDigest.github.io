On September 12, 1962, one of the most famous speeches in history was delivered by President John F. Kennedy at Rice University in Houston, Texas. In his speech, known as the "We choose to go to the Moon" speech, Kennedy set the ambitious goal of sending a man to the Moon and safely returning him to Earth before the end of the decade. This iconic address ignited America's space program and led to the successful Apollo lunar missions, culminating in the historic Moon landing in 1969.
