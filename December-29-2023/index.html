FUN FACT ON THIS DAY:

On this day, December 29th, in 1934, the Italian luxury automobile manufacturer, Lancia, introduced their groundbreaking Lancia Lambda model at the Turin Motor Show. The Lancia Lambda was a significant innovation in the automotive industry as it was the first car to feature an all-independent suspension, which greatly improved comfort and handling for drivers and passengers alike. Its advanced design set new standards for automobile engineering and paved the way for modern suspension systems used in vehicles today.

🌎 WORLD NEWS

Ukraine war: Wave of Russian air strikes reported across Ukraine

-BBC.com

1. In a widespread overnight assault, Russia launched a wave of attacks on several cities in Ukraine, including Kyiv, Odesa, Dnipropetrovsk, Kharkiv, and Lviv.

2. At least 12 people have been killed and a number injured in the attacks, which targeted social and critical infrastructure across Ukraine.

3. Russian missiles, including difficult-to-intercept types, were used in the attacks, causing significant damage to buildings, including a metro station acting as an air raid shelter, warehouses, and residential buildings.

Israel-Hamas war: Tens of thousands of Palestinians stream into Rafah

-The Associated Press

1. Over 20 people were killed in an Israeli airstrike that destroyed a residential building in Rafah, Gaza Strip.

2. Tens of thousands of Palestinians have been forced to flee their homes due to Israel's bombardment, seeking shelter in overcrowded areas.

3. The majority of Gaza's population is dependent on humanitarian aid, and the ongoing conflict has severely restricted access to essential supplies.

North Korea says it is preparing for war with the U.S.

-NPR

1. North Korea is claiming to be preparing for war with the United States in response to what Kim Jong Un calls unprecedented, confrontational moves by the U.S.

2. The rhetoric from North Korea is consistent with their complaints about U.S. and South Korean military exercises and nuclear consultations, which they see as a practice for war.

3. While the actual threat level of North Korea's military capabilities is debatable, the geographic proximity and the enshrining of the nuclear weapons program into law make the situation complex and difficult to convince North Korea to give up its nuclear weapons.

-Deadline
